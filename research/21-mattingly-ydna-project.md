## 1. Project Overview

The **Mattingly-Matney Y-DNA Surname Project** is hosted at FamilyTreeDNA under the group name "Mattingly-Matney." As of April 2025 the project has **53 members**. The stated subtitle is "The Paternal Descendants of Thomas Mattingly I of Maryland." Administrators are Robert Williams and Steve Hank (co-administrator). Project fund balance: $755.05.

## 2. Founding Lineage

Thomas Mattingly I emigrated from England around 1663 with wife Elizabeth (maiden unknown), arriving in St. Mary's County, Maryland. He died 24 Jul 1664, MD. His two sons who carried the Y-chromosome forward:

| Son | Born | Died | Line continues as |
|-----|------|------|-------------------|
| Thomas Mattingly II | ~1650, England | 12 Jan 1714, MD | "Thomas II line" — SNPs Y20213, Y20214 |
| Cezar "Cesar" Mattingly | ~1654, England | 1719, MD | "Cezar line" — SNP Y14083 |

The project also covers the surname variant **Matney**, documented as a derivative of Mattingly. DNA confirms that nearly all Matney participants descend from Cezar (they carry Y14083 but not Y20213/Y20214).

## 3. Haplogroup: R1b-DF27 > Y14084

### 3a. Placement in the Y-tree

The full phylogenetic path from root to the Mattingly clade:

```
R1b > R-L754 > R-L761 > R-L389 > R-P297 > R-M269 > R-L23 > R-L51 > R-L52
  > R-PF6538 > R-L151 > R-P312 > R-DF27 > R-Y32298 > R-FT56526 > R-Y14084
```

The project's Blatchford match page specifies the intermediate path as:
`R1b-DF27 > S250 > ZZ12_1 > FGC78762 > ZZ19_1 > Y32298 > FT56526 > Y14084*`

Source: https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Space:Mattingly-Blatchford_DNA_Match

### 3b. R-Y14084 Age Estimates (YFull)

Per YFull haplotree https://www.yfull.com/tree/R-Y14084/ (accessed 2026-04-25):

| Metric | Point estimate | 95% CI |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Clade formed | 2400 ybp (approx. 374 BCE) | 3500 to 1400 ybp |
| TMRCA (all living members) | 350 ybp (approx. 1676 CE) | 500 to 225 ybp |

The TMRCA of ~350 ybp (roughly 1600–1700 CE) is consistent with Thomas Mattingly I as the most recent common ancestor of the American Mattingly branch — he arrived in Maryland around 1663.

### 3c. Defining SNPs for R-Y14084

YFull lists the haplogroup-defining SNPs as: `Y18575 * Y18580 * Y18578` (primary markers), plus 22 additional SNPs including: `Y18576 * Y18577 * A23315 * A23316 * Y226158(H) * Y170844/FT55233 * Y14084 * Y14085 * Y13607 * Y505272(H) * Y14081 * A23314 * Y13608 * Y14080(H) * Y14082 * Y14086 * Y18449 * Y18574 * Y172388 * Y13609 * Y18581 * Y18579`

The project's SNP table (source: https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Space:Mattingly-Matney_SNPs) lists 46 named SNPs in total, categorized by which descendants carry them.

### 3d. Sub-branches of R-Y14084 (YFull, as of April 2025)

YFull shows the following immediate child clades of R-Y14084 with their TMRCA estimates and geographic distribution of test participants:

| Subclade | Formed (ybp) | TMRCA (ybp) | Samples in YFull | Geography |
|----------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|

**Key observation:** The majority of R-Y14084 YFull samples cluster in UK Hampshire — the ancestral Mattingley village area — confirming the surname's origin. US samples concentrate in Virginia and Kentucky, consistent with the documented Maryland > Virginia > Kentucky migration pattern.

The subclade **R-Y14083** is the Cezar Mattingly marker. The project identifies:
- **R-Y80210**: Stephen Matney and Uriah Allen Matney descendants
- **R-Y85450**: Walter Matney Jr. and Broadwater Matney descendants

The subclade **R-Y20214** carries the Thomas II marker. Project sub-SNPs within Thomas II descendants:
- **Y20213**: William Taylor Mattingly descendants only
- **Y20214**: Leonard Mattingly Jr. descendants only
- **Y30438**: Leonard Mattingly Jr. descendants only (co-occurs with Y20214)

## 5. Famous Mattinglys and Y-DNA

### Don Mattingly (baseball player, b. 1961)

Don Mattingly (born April 20, 1961, Evansville, Indiana) is the most famous bearer of the Mattingly surname. His father is Bill Mattingly. He grew up in Evansville, Indiana, attended Reitz Memorial High School, and was drafted by the Yankees in 1979.

**Y-DNA status: No public test on record.** Don Mattingly has not published Y-DNA results through FTDNA, YFull, or any genealogical project as of April 2025. His name does not appear in the Mattingly-Matney project participant lists.

**Genealogical connection to Maryland line: Unconfirmed.** Many Indiana Mattinglys trace their ancestry to the Kentucky Mattinglys who migrated from Maryland. Evansville, Indiana is in Vanderburgh County in southwestern Indiana — a geographic corridor consistent with the Kentucky > Indiana migration path used by multiple Mattingly families in the early 1800s. However, no direct paper trail connecting Don Mattingly's paternal line to Thomas Mattingly I has been publicly documented. His father Bill Mattingly's ancestry from Evansville has not been linked to a specific branch in the project literature.

**What Y-DNA could reveal:** If Don Mattingly were to test, a positive result for Y14084 would place him in the Thomas I > Thomas II or Cezar branches. A positive for Y20214 would specifically link him to the Leonard Mattingly Jr. line. This remains untested.

## 7. Connection to Leonard Sr. (1739) and Ignatius (1704)

The project has confirmed DNA participants tracing to this direct line:

```
Thomas Mattingly I (d. 1664, MD) [R-Y14084*]
  └── Thomas Mattingly II (~1650–1714, MD)
        └── Ignatius Mattingly Sr. (abt.1704–1789, St. Mary's Co., MD) [WikiTree: Mattingly-60]
              └── Leonard "Old Leonard" Mattingly (1739–abt.1829, St. Mary's Co., MD) [WikiTree: Mattingly-62]
                    ├── T1, T2, T3 — confirmed Y-DNA participants (SNPs Y20214 + Y30438)
                    └── Leonard Mattingly Jr. (1764–1843, Maryland > Kentucky)
                          └── [Line continues to present-day Kentucky/Texas Mattinglys]
```

**Confirmed SNP evidence for this line:**
- Y20214 (position 15975373, G>C) = Leonard Mattingly Jr. descendants ONLY
- Y30438 (position 13669609) = Leonard Mattingly Jr. descendants ONLY — co-occurs with Y20214

**What is needed to extend coverage:**
1. A Y-DNA test from any male-line descendant of Leonard Jr. other than through the already-tested branches (T1, T2, T3 are cousins through Leonard Jr.)
2. A descendant of **William "Constable Billy" Mattingly** (another son of Thomas II, sibling to Ignatius) — not yet in the project
3. A descendant of any son of Leonard Sr. other than Leonard Jr. — the project specifically calls this out in its notice

**Ignatius Mattingly Sr. (1704) path to our lineage:** The project uses WikiTree ID Mattingly-60 for Ignatius Sr. His son Leonard Sr. (1739) is Mattingly-62. The Thomas II > Ignatius > Leonard > Leonard Jr. chain is fully documented and DNA-confirmed through T1/T2/T3 participants.

## 9. Key Gaps and Research Priorities

1. **Leonard Sr. (1739) extended coverage:** Only descendants of Leonard Jr. (one of several sons) are currently tested. Descendants through other sons of Leonard Sr. — Joseph, John Baptist, Ignatius Jr., Basil, Susannah's male line, etc. — remain untested.

2. **Ignatius Mattingly Jr. (T5) branch:** Only one participant currently. Additional descendants needed to identify any new sub-SNPs.

3. **William "Constable Billy" Mattingly:** Zero participants representing this Thomas II son. The project explicitly requests one.

4. **British Mattingley families:** The project requests UK Mattingley males to test. YFull shows Hampshire-origin samples in R-FT61324, R-FTG32794, R-Y14083, R-Y200586, and R-Y97568 — these likely include Mattingley surname holders who predate the American emigration.

5. **Don Mattingly:** No Y-DNA test on record. His Evansville, Indiana origin is geographically consistent with the Kentucky Mattingly migration corridor but the genealogical bridge is not documented.

6. **Brooks Matney line:** Project notice asks whether descendants of Brooks Matney carry R1b-DF97* (a different haplogroup from Y14084 entirely — suggesting possible NPE or unrelated Matney origin).
