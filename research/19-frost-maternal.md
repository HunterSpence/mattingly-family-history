## The Core Hypothesis (PROBABLE, UNCONFIRMED)

Shari's mother was a daughter of T.C. Frost III and Ilse Herff, making:
- Her **maiden name: Frost**
- Her brother: **Tom Frost Jr.** — the confirmed bank president
- Her birth year: likely **~1925-1927** (not 1923 — see note below)

**Why probable:** The oral family tradition of "uncle was president of Frost Bank" maps precisely onto Tom Frost Jr.'s confirmed role. No other accessible Frost family branch produces a plausible bank president in the right era.

**Why unconfirmed:** No accessible source names a daughter of T.C. Frost III born in that window. Find a Grave lists only two children. No Frost-Mattingly marriage record was located. The entire link rests on family oral tradition plus the circumstantial fit of Tom Frost Jr.

### Birth Year Note

T.C. Frost III was born December 1903. Ilse Herff was born June 1906. Their marriage date was not found in accessible records but was most likely ~1924-1926 (upper-class San Antonio families of that era typically married in their early 20s). A 1923 birth would require T.C. Frost III to father a child at age 19 and Ilse Herff at age 17, before their likely marriage — possible but inconsistent with their social milieu. Birth year ~1926-1927 is more plausible; the family oral tradition figure of 1923 may carry a 3-4 year error.

## Eliminated Candidate

**Joseph Hardin Frost Jr.** (1915-1971, Find a Grave 22626948) is listed on Find a Grave as "business investor" — not a bank president. He was a son of Joseph H. Frost + Eda Kampmann, a different branch. He does not fit the oral tradition.

## How to Resolve This Research

The critical gap requires one of three paths:

**Path A (fastest) — Ask the family.** Shari Mattingly herself, or any living Mattingly relative, may know her mother's maiden name. That one data point would confirm or refute the entire Frost hypothesis.

**Path B — Ancestry.com subscription.** Texas Birth Index, Texas Marriage Index, and Texas Death Index are all indexed on Ancestry. A one-month subscription (~$25) would likely resolve questions 1-4 above within hours.

**Path C — San Antonio Public Library / Bexar County records.** The Texana/Genealogy Department at SAPL (600 Soledad, San Antonio) holds microfilmed Express-News archives and Bexar County vital records. Remote access to some records is available via mail request to the Bexar County District Clerk.
