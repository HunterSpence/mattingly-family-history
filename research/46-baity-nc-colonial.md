# Baity / Pearl Baity / North Carolina-to-Texas Colonial Side

**Prepared:** 2026-04-26  
**Researcher:** Codex parallel worker  
**Scope:** direct Baity-side research only; no updates to `entities.json` or build artifacts  
**Confidence tiers used here:** `CONFIRMED` = directly supported by a cited record in this pass; `PROBABLE` = strong fit but one generational link still needs a census/probate/deed image; `POSSIBLE` = plausible line, not yet safe to treat as proved

---

## Executive Summary

The defensible Baity line, using records I could actually put under citation in this pass, runs most safely as:

1. **Paralee "Pearl" Baity** of San Antonio, Texas (`CONFIRMED` as a married Baity woman in San Antonio by 1910)
2. wife of **W. A. Baity** (`CONFIRMED` as her husband in Bexar County land records)
3. **William Alexander Baity** of Yadkin County, North Carolina (`PROBABLE` identification from prior repo work; this pass independently re-found the Texas land traces but not the vital-record image)
4. probable son of **William D. Baity** (1829/1830-1894, Courtney Baptist Cemetery, Yadkin County) (`PROBABLE`)
5. probable son of **Isham "Isom" Baity** (19 Oct 1804-23 Dec 1892, Courtney Baptist Cemetery) (`PROBABLE`)
6. probable/possible son of **David Baity** (adult in Surry County by 1830) (`POSSIBLE/PROBABLE`)
7. probable/possible son of **George Baity / Batee / Baty** (adult in Rowan County by **May 6, 1774**) (`POSSIBLE`)

Two bottom lines matter:

- I **did not find a verifiable immigrant arrival** for the direct Baity line.  
- The **earliest defensible Baity presence is already colonial North Carolina**, with George Baity/Batee/Baty appearing in Rowan/Surry records from **1774 forward**.

That means the "colonial side" is real, but the **pre-North-Carolina jump is still unproved**.

## 1. Pearl / Paralee Baity in Texas

### What is solid

- Shari's transcript says her great-grandmother **Pearl Baity** came from **North Carolina to Texas as a child**, later bought **Reeves County land in April 1901**, and retained the mineral rights when she sold the surface.  
  Source: [transcripts/final.md](../transcripts/final.md)

- A Bexar County archival description confirms that **"W. A. Baity and Paralee Baity"** conveyed San Antonio land on **November 2, 1910**.  
  Source: East Texas Research Center, Stephen F. Austin State University, "Deed: W. A. Baity and Paralee Baity to W. F. Daniel, E. D. Wintz and J. T. Gallant, Bexar County, Texas, land in San Antonio, November 2, 1910"  
  https://archives.sfasu.edu/repositories/2/archival_objects/19330

- Portal to Texas History search snippets show the same couple in Bexar County real-estate notices in **1910** and **1925**, which independently supports that the Baitys were active property holders in San Antonio:
  - *San Antonio Light and Gazette*, **May 30, 1910**, page 8  
    https://texashistory.unt.edu/ark%3A/67531/metapth1692586/m1/8/
  - *San Antonio Light*, **October 16, 1925**, page 33  
    https://texashistory.unt.edu/ark%3A/67531/metapth1593025/m1/33/

### What remains weaker than I want

- Prior repo work identifies "Pearl" as **Paralee Johnson Baity** and identifies her husband as **William Alexander Baity**. I did **not** independently re-pull the Grayson County marriage record or the Texas death certificate image in this pass, so I am treating that exact identification as `PROBABLE`, not `CONFIRMED`, here.

- A low-grade modern death-index aggregator gives **Paralee Baity, born 26 Jul 1878, died Dec 1969, San Antonio**. That is directionally useful but not strong enough to stand alone.  
  Source: AncientFaces surname directory snippet  
  https://www.ancientfaces.com/surname/baity-family-history/410608

### Reeves County / mineral-rights story

- The **oral-history claim** is clear and consistent inside the transcript.
- The **geology claim** is true: the Wolfcamp/Spraberry-Wolfcamp play in Reeves County became one of the major modern oil targets, and the USGS 2016 assessment was the headline event behind the "largest assessment" language.  
  Source: USGS Fact Sheet 2016-3092  
  https://pubs.usgs.gov/fs/2016/3092/fs20163092.pdf

I did **not** recover the original **April 1901 Reeves County purchase instrument** in this pass. I would treat the land/mineral story as **credible oral history with modern geological corroboration**, but the original purchase deed or GLO file still needs to be pulled before treating it as fully proved.

## 2. The Best-Supported North Carolina Line

### A. William D. Baity (1829/1830-1894)

**Status:** `PROBABLE` direct-line ancestor

Why he matters:

- Prior project work already pointed to **William D. Baity** as the father-generation immediately above William Alexander.
- A cemetery transcription places **William D. Baity** at **Courtney Baptist Church Cemetery, Yadkin County**, with dates **1830-1894**.  
  Source: Yadkin County cemetery census  
  https://www.cemeterycensus.org/nc/yadk/cem049.htm

- A burial-index aggregation independently echoes **William D Baity (1829-1894)** at the same cemetery.  
  Source: PeopleLegacy burial index  
  https://peoplelegacy.com/burial-records/william/baity/

Assessment:

- I am comfortable saying a real deceased **William D. Baity** existed in the right place and time.
- I am **not** comfortable calling the William-D-to-William-Alexander parent-child link `CONFIRMED` until the **1870/1880 census** or a probate/deed record is cited directly.

### B. Isham "Isom" Baity (19 Oct 1804-23 Dec 1892)

**Status:** `PROBABLE` direct-line ancestor

Why he matters:

- Courtney Baptist Church Cemetery lists **Isham "Isom" Baity** with exact dates **19 Oct 1804 - 23 Dec 1892**.  
  Source: cemetery census  
  https://www.cemeterycensus.org/nc/yadk/cem049.htm

- A separate cemetery-census snippet identifies **Mary Jane Baity** as a **daughter of Isham Baity and Nancy Plowman**, which is useful evidence that the Isham/Nancy household is a real documented family unit in this neighborhood.  
  Source: Deep Creek Baptist Church cemetery snippet  
  https://www.cemeterycensus.org/nc/yadk/cem026.htm

Assessment:

- Isham is a real, well-placed Yadkin/Surry-era Baity ancestor.
- The step from **William D. Baity to Isham Baity** still needs a directly cited census or estate record, but it fits the local cemetery cluster and the prior project's candidate line well enough to call `PROBABLE`.

### C. David Baity (adult by 1830)

**Status:** `POSSIBLE/PROBABLE`

Why he matters:

- The **1830 Surry County census** includes separate heads of household named **David Baity**, **George Baity**, **Isam Baity**, and **Pleasant Baity**.  
  Source: NCGenWeb transcription of the 1830 census  
  https://www.ncgenweb.us/alleghany/surry1830.html

Assessment:

- This is the strongest public clue that the family cluster was already in Surry County by 1830.
- It does **not**, by itself, prove that David was Isham's father, but it makes the older-project candidate line plausible.
- I would keep **David -> Isham** at `POSSIBLE/PROBABLE` until a deed, estate, or 1850 census household link is cited directly.

### D. George Baity / Batee / Baty (adult by 1774)

**Status:** `POSSIBLE`

Why he matters:

- A public Ancestry-sharing page reproduces a useful summary of earlier Rowan/Surry references for **George Baity / Batee / Baty**:
  - **May 6, 1774**: "George Beate vs. Hugh Montgomery" in Rowan County Inferior Court
  - **April 2, 1785**: bond from Sylvester and John Thomson to sell George Batee **124 acres on Dutchman's Creek**
  - **1787**: George Batey paying Rowan County taxes
  - **January 19, 1798**: Thomas Horn to George Baty, **300 acres on Chiquapin Creek** near the Rowan line
  - **1799-1800**: David and William Batey taxed in the same district
  - **1800**: George Beaty in the census  
  Source: Ancestry-shared story snippet for George C. Baity  
  https://www.ancestry.com/sharing/21081190?h=af0454

Assessment:

- I would **not** treat the above as fully proved until those entries are rechecked in the original Rowan/Surry court, tax, deed, and census books.
- But it is enough to say the Baity line is **documented in colonial/revolutionary-era North Carolina by 1774**.

## 3. Military and Civic Records Near the Direct Line

These are not all direct ancestors, but they are clearly part of the same Yadkin Baity network and are useful context.

### Confederate service

- **Pleasant H. Baity**, resident of Yadkin County, enlisted as a private in **Company I, 28th North Carolina Infantry** on **March 8, 1862**, age 18.  
- **William W. Baity**, also in Company I, enlisted on **August 13, 1861** and **died of disease on December 8, 1862** at Winchester, Virginia; burial is given as **Stonewall Confederate Cemetery, grave 328**.  
  Source: MilitaryHistoryOnline genealogy transcription  
  https://www.militaryhistoryonline.com/Genealogy/Regiment/North%20Carolina/4/2389

- The official **North Carolina Troops Roster Index** also lists Baity soldiers including **I. H. Baity**, **Pleasant H. Baity**, and **William D., Sr.**  
  Source: North Carolina Department of Natural and Cultural Resources roster index PDF  
  https://www.dncr.nc.gov/documents/files/civil-war-roster-may-2020/download

Assessment:

- The Yadkin Baity family clearly had Civil War service.  
- For direct-line purposes, this is strongest as **network evidence** around Isham/William-D-era Baitys.

### Land / property records in San Antonio

- **W. A. and Paralee Baity** appear in **Bexar County land transactions** in 1910 and 1925.  
- That fits the oral history that this branch became prosperous in San Antonio real estate/building circles, even if I did not independently recover a contractor-license or business record in this pass.

## 4. Colonial-Side Conclusion

### What I would state now

- The Baity branch is **solidly North Carolina before 1800**.
- The earliest defensible ancestor in this pass is **George Baity/Batee/Baty**, active in Rowan County records by **May 6, 1774**.
- The line **George -> David -> Isham -> William D. -> William Alexander -> W.A./Paralee Texas branch** is **plausible and probably right**, but only the lower Texas couple and the cemetery-anchored North Carolina generations are near-documentary strength right now.

### What I would *not* state yet

- I would **not** claim a specific immigrant ancestor.
- I would **not** claim George Baity's parents.
- I would **not** claim Rachel Allgood as a proved wife of George for direct-line purposes without a better marriage, deed, probate, or child-link record.
