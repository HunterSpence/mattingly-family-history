# Hans Teichmueller — German Ancestry Research

**Research date:** 2026-04-25
**Researcher:** Claude Sonnet 4.6 (Researcher specialist)

---

## Anchor Confirmed

**Hans Teichmueller** — born 7 March 1837, Brunswick (Braunschweig), Duchy of Brunswick; died 17 February 1901, La Grange, Fayette County, Texas. District Judge of the 22nd Judicial District (1884-1901). Buried: La Grange Cemetery.

Primary source text (Lotto 1902, p.229-231, digitized at archive.org/details/fayettecountyher00lott):

> "Hans Teichmueller comes from a respected family. His parents were August and Charlotte Teichmueller, nee von Gursewald. His father was an officer in the Brunswick army. Hans Teichmueller was born on March 7, 1837 at Brunswick, Germany, and attended the public schools of that city and the gymnasium of Blankenburg, where he graduated."

## Hans's Parents — CONFIRMED (primary source)

### Father: August Teichmüller

- **Name:** August Teichmüller
- **Occupation:** Officer in the Duchy of Brunswick army ("pensionierter Soldat" / retired soldier per Basel Univ. archive; "lieutenant in the Prussian army" per English Wikipedia — the 1902 Lotto primary source specifying "Brunswick army" is preferred)
- **Death:** Before 1856; death caused family financial hardship prompting both sons to abandon planned careers. Gustav (born 1832) turned to tutoring; Hans (born 1837) emigrated to Texas.
- **Birth/death dates:** Not found in accessible digitized sources
- **Source:** Lotto 1902 (primary); independently confirmed in Wikipedia articles on son Gustav Teichmüller

### Mother: Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth von Girsewaldt (married: Teichmüller)

- **Maiden name:** von Girsewaldt (English Wikipedia); von Girsewald (German sources); von Gursewald (Lotto 1902 OCR artifact of the same name)
- **Full name:** Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth — from English Wikipedia article on son Gustav, citing Basel University source (now 404; Wayback archive 2011 confirms "seine Mutter stammte aus einer Offiziersfamilie" = "his mother came from an officer's family")
- **Family:** The von Girsewald are documented Braunschweig nobles, elevated to Freiherren in 1827. Charlotte's exact position within the family is UNVERIFIED in accessible online sources (see section below).
- **Confidence:** PROBABLE (maiden name confirmed by two independent Wikipedia articles); POSSIBLE (full forenames)

## The Key Discovery: Gustav Teichmüller is Hans's Brother — CONFIRMED

**Gustav Teichmüller** (19 November 1832, Braunschweig — 22 May 1888, Dorpat/Tartu, Estonia) was a distinguished German philosopher whose works influenced Friedrich Nietzsche's perspectivism. He was a professor at the University of Basel (1868-1871) and the Imperial University of Dorpat (1871-1888).

Wikipedia (English) states explicitly: "He was the son of August Teichmüller and Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth Teichmüller, née von Girsewaldt. His father was a lieutenant in the Prussian army."

German Wikipedia confirms: "Durch den Tod des Vaters in finanzielle Schwierigkeiten geraten" — "Reduced to financial difficulties by the death of his father" — exactly matching the Lotto 1902 account of why Hans emigrated.

Hans was born 1837; Gustav was born 1832. The TSHA says Hans was "the third son," meaning at least one brother exists between them (unnamed in all accessible sources).

Gustav's Wikipedia entry: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav_Teichm%C3%BCller

## The Von Girsewald Family (Charlotte's Maternal Line)

The von Girsewald family of Braunschweig are documented Prussian/Brunswick nobles with German Wikipedia articles on three members:

| Person | Birth | Death | Rank |
|--------|-------|-------|------|
| Conrad Alexander Gustav von Girsewald (Freiherr 1827) | 1785, Braunschweig | 1864, Braunschweig | Generalmajor, Oberstallmeister, Kammerherr |
| Gustav Wilhelm Heinrich Ernst von Girsewald | 1812, York, Ireland | 1871, Braunschweig | Generalmajor, Infantry Regiment commander |
| Alexander von Girsewald | 1815 | 1890 | Generalmajor, Oberstallmeister |
| Wilhelm von Girsewald | ~1785 | unknown | Braunschweig army; listed in Braunschweig Biog. Lexikon I p.211 |

The 1785 Gustav (the senior Freiherr) married Katharina Raines (1787-1857) and had sons Gustav (1812) and Alexander (1815) — no daughters documented in Wikipedia. His brother Wilhelm is mentioned in the 1809 Dörnberg uprising account and appears in the Braunschweig Biographical Lexicon but has no Wikipedia article. Charlotte was likely from this Wilhelm branch or another undocumented line.

**Key gap:** The Gothaisches Genealogisches Taschenbuch der Freiherrlichen Häuser (1921 edition, p.298) covers the full von Girsewald Freiherrliche family tree and would resolve Charlotte's parentage. This source is cited in both Wikipedia articles but is not digitized online. Access via a German archive (Niedersächsisches Landesarchiv, NLA, Wolfenbüttel or Hannover) would be required.

**Charlotte's birth estimate:** ~1800-1815 (to have borne Gustav in 1832 and Hans in 1837). If from the Wilhelm branch, Wilhelm would have been Charlotte's father — born ~1780-1790, consistent with the family's documented generation.

## Hans's Wife — CONFIRMED

**Augusta Dorthea Elizabeth Kellner** (born 25 Mar 1843; died 9 Jul 1915)

- Father: "a highly educated teacher of modern languages" at Postoak Point, Austin County, Texas — this teacher taught Hans English when Hans first arrived in Fayette County (TSHA). Name not recorded in any accessible source.
- Birth place: WikiTree records "Germany"; TSHA implies Texas-born. The discrepancy is unresolved.
- **Marriage date:** 10 June 1858 (Lotto 1902 primary source — "June 10, 1858" confirmed by direct text search of the digitized book). NOTE: The TSHA (Biesele, 1952) says "June 16, 1858" — this is a transcription error; the Lotto 1902 primary clearly reads June 10.
- **Marriage place:** Postoak Point, Austin County, Texas
- **Officiant:** Colonel Henderson, then justice of the peace

## Hans's Children — CONFIRMED

Five children total (Lotto 1902). Four named in accessible records:

1. **Louisa Teichmueller** (abt. Sep 1862 – abt. Aug 1870) — died before age 8; appears in 1870 census, absent thereafter. WikiTree Teichmueller-2.

2. **Mary (May) Teichmueller Mattingly** (16 Dec 1867 – 28 Aug 1900, La Grange) — CRITICAL ANCESTOR. Married **Edward Mattingly Sr.** on 1 September 1894 at La Grange. Her husband was assistant cashier of the First National Bank of La Grange. She died at age 32, two years before the Lotto book was published. Children: Claude Mattingly, **Leroy Teichmueller Mattingly** (b.1898, named for grandfather Hans), Mamie Mattingly. WikiTree Teichmueller-1 / Find A Grave #57057870.

3. **Minette (Minnetta) Teichmueller Pohl** (27 Jan 1871 – 28 Aug 1970, Nacogdoches) — Muralist; WPA Federal Art Project artist. Known for "The Law, Texas Rangers" mural at Smithville, TX post office. Married E.R. (Earl P.) Richardson 22 Jan 1913; later married Hugo Pohl. Lived to age 99. Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minette_Teichmueller

4. **Anna E. (Annie Elizabeth) Teichmueller** (28 Nov 1874 – 13 Jan 1961, San Antonio) — Never married. Lived with sister Minette in San Antonio after 1920. Texas death certificate #00424. WikiTree Teichmueller-5.

5. **Fifth child** — unnamed in all accessible sources. Lotto 1902 states "five children" total; the 1880 census lists the household but a fifth child between Minette (b.1871) and Anna (b.1874) or before Louisa (b.1862) may be the unnamed one. POSSIBLE, not confirmed.

## Hans's Emigration — CONFIRMED

- **Year:** 1856
- **Reason:** Death of father August left the family in financial straits; Hans abandoned a planned theology career
- **Route:** Brunswick → sailed to New York as a cabin boy (~age 14-15, before graduation, returned to Germany) → emigrated permanently 1856
- **First contact in Texas:** "Old man Fordtrau of Industry" (Austin County) gave him advice about country conditions — Industry was founded by German settlers in 1831
- **Cousin in Texas:** Arthur Meerscheidt, who had a farm in Fayette County — TSHA confirms "he came to Texas to join his cousin, Arthur Meerscheidt." Meerscheidt later became Hans's law partner.
- **Settlement sequence:** Shelby → Postoak Point → High Hill (1861, farmed) → New Ulm (taught school during Civil War) → La Grange (1866 onward, law practice until death)

## What Remains Unverified (German Records)

1. **August Teichmüller's birth/death dates, parents, and rank** — Niedersächsisches Landesarchiv (NLA, Wolfenbüttel/Hannover) holds Brunswick civil and church records; Matricula Online has some digitized church registers but the relevant Braunschweig Lutheran parish records for 1800-1860 are not confirmed online.

2. **Charlotte von Girsewald's parents** — Gothaisches Genealogisches Taschenbuch der Freiherrlichen Häuser (1921, p.298) is the key source. NLA or Wolfenbüttel State Archive would hold the records.

3. **Teichmüller grandparents (August's parents)** — No source found. German surname "Teichmüller" is not uncommon; without August's birth parish, cannot trace further back.

4. **The unnamed second son** (between Gustav and Hans) — TSHA says "third son," implying this person existed. No name in any source.

5. **The fifth Texas child** — Lotto 1902 says five children; only four are named in accessible records.

6. **1908 biographical sketch** — "Hans Teichmueller, Biographical Sketch" (Austin: Von Boeckmann-Jones, 1908). Cited in TSHA bibliography. Not digitized. UT Austin (Briscoe Center) or Texas State Library likely holds a copy. This may name additional family details.
