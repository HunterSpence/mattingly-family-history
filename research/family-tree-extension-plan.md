## Confirmed sources of leverage

The Maryland Catholic Mattingly family has been heavily researched for over a century. We are not building from scratch.

### 1. FamilySearch (free, primary)

- The **Mattingly surname** has thousands of profiles in FamilySearch's collaborative tree
- Catholic Maryland Mattinglys have particularly dense research because parish baptismal/marriage/burial records survived continuously from the 1660s
- **Search URL:** https://www.familysearch.org/search/record/results?q.surname=Mattingly&q.birthLikePlace=Maryland
- **Tree view:** https://www.familysearch.org/tree/find/name?birth=1623&birthRange=1620-1626&givenName=Thomas&surname=Mattingly
- Will give us most of generations 2–7 directly

### 2. WikiTree (free, vetted)

- Has a **Mattingly Name Study** with manually curated, well-sourced entries
- Profiles are public, sources cited, generation links explicit
- **Browse:** https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Mattingly-1
- Particularly strong on the Maryland → Kentucky transitions

### 3. Maryland State Archives

- Land records, court records, wills for Charles County (where Mattingly's Hope is located)
- **MSA database:** https://msa.maryland.gov/megafile/msa/speccol/sc4400/sc4404/
- Plats database for the Mattingly's Hope patent and subsequent re-patents

### 4. Catholic parish registers

- St. Inigoes, St. Mary's City, Newport, Bryantown — the main 17th–18th c. Catholic Maryland parishes
- Many digitized via FamilySearch and the Catholic Heritage Council

### 5. The Mattingly genealogy literature

- **"The Mattingly Family in America"** by Charles Mattingly (multiple editions, 20th c.) — covers the Maryland Catholic line through ~1900
- **"Maryland Catholics on the Frontier"** (Catholic University Press) — covers the 1785 Bardstown migration
- **Kentucky Catholic Heritage** publications — the 1785 cohort is named individual-by-individual

### 6. Texas genealogy resources

- **Portal to Texas History:** https://texashistory.unt.edu/
- **Texas State Library and Archives:** https://www.tsl.texas.gov/
- **Briscoe Center for American History (UT Austin):** holds San Antonio Express-News microfilm archive — critical for the Centenarian (1935 obituary), the Texas Judge, and Claude Mattingly's 1934 death

## Research strategy by generation

### Generations 1–2 (1623–1700) — well documented

✅ **Already mostly done.** Thomas I (1623–1665) and Thomas II + Cezar (1666 patent) are confirmed. Need: Thomas I's English origin town (most likely Hampshire, given the Mattingley village).

### Generations 3–6 (1690–1800) — Maryland Catholic period

🔍 **The middle problem.** Five generations in Charles County, Maryland between 1666 and ~1810. Strategy:

1. Pull Thomas II's children from FamilySearch (likely 5–8 children given the era)
2. Identify which child carried the line forward — this is where we trace through known descendant connections back to the centenarian
3. Catholic baptismal records will name parents → grandparents → great-grandparents continuously
4. Cross-check WikiTree's Mattingly Name Study for confirmed parent–child links

### Generation 6–7 (1780s–1810s) — the Bardstown KY split

🔍 **The migration question.** The 1785 Maryland-Catholic-to-Kentucky migration is well documented. The question is whether the Texas line:

- (a) **Stayed in Maryland** then migrated south to Texas later, OR
- (b) **Went to Kentucky** with the 1785 cohort, then south to Texas

Shari said "we also had relatives who followed Daniel Boone... they're still in Boonesborough" — implying *collateral* Mattinglys went to Kentucky while *her direct line* may have stayed in Maryland or taken a different route.

### Generation 8 (1828–1935) — The Centenarian

🔍 **Strong anchor.** A man who lived from 1828 to 1935 (age 107) is statistically rare and likely well documented. Strategy:

- Search Texas (and possibly Kentucky/Maryland depending on where he lived) newspapers ~1928 for "100th birthday" coverage
- His 1935 death almost certainly produced a substantial obituary
- US Census records 1830, 1840, 1850, 1860, 1870, 1880, 1900, 1910, 1920, 1930 will all contain him
- His name is the missing link — once found, generations 7 and 9 connect immediately

### Generation 9 — The Texas Judge

🔍 **Searchable.** A Texas judge in the late 19th century is documented in:

- **Texas Bar Association historical rolls**
- **County court records** (he held a judicial office, county-level minimum)
- **San Antonio newspapers** if he served in Bexar County
- **Marriage records** to a Teichmueller (German-Texan family) — La Grange / Fayette County is the heartland

### Generation 10 — Leroy Teichmuller Mattingly (b. 1898)

✅ **Mostly known.** Anchored in:

- University of Texas alumni records (engineering, ~1920s graduate)
- 1900, 1910, 1920, 1930, 1940 US Census
- Texas marriage records for his marriage
- Death records for his date of death (Shari didn't say)

### Generations 11–13 — Living memory

✅ **Hunter knows or can ask.** Shari + David, their children, Hunter's parents.

## Three-tier confidence rules

| Tier | Standard |
|------|----------|
| **CONFIRMED** | 3+ independent primary sources agree on parent–child link |
| **PROBABLE** | 2 sources, no contradicting evidence |
| **POSSIBLE** | 1 source or strong circumstantial / dating consistency |
| **UNVERIFIED** | Inference only — flagged in tree as dashed border |

The current D3 tree on the family-history page color-codes these:
- Solid dark border = CONFIRMED
- Solid light border = PROBABLE
- Dashed border = UNVERIFIED / placeholder
