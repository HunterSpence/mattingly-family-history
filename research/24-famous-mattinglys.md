## Summary

This report covers 12 notable persons sharing the Mattingly or Teichmueller surname, assessed for genealogical proximity to Hunter Spence's paternal and maternal lines. The single most significant new finding is that **Gustav Teichmuller (1832–1888)**, philosopher who influenced Nietzsche, almost certainly shares the same parents as **Hans Teichmueller (1837–1901)**, Hunter's maternal great-great-grandfather — making Gustav Hunter's great-great-great-granduncle.

A second major finding: **Marie Mattingly Meloney (1878–1943)**, the journalist who organized the 1921 radium fundraiser for Marie Curie, was born in Bardstown, Kentucky — the exact Catholic Mattingly clan seat — to a physician father named Cyprian Peter Mattingly, placing her firmly in the same patrilineal descent as Hunter's Texas Mattingly line.

## Section 1: Mattingly Notables

### 1. Don Mattingly (1961–present)
**Claim:** MLB Hall of Fame-caliber first baseman, NY Yankees 1982–1995. Six All-Star appearances, 1985 AL MVP, nine Gold Gloves. Career .307 batting average. Later managed the LA Dodgers (2011–2015) and Miami Marlins (2016–2022); currently bench coach for the Philadelphia Phillies. His #23 is retired by the Yankees.

**Lineage assessment:** Born Evansville, Indiana. Father: William "Bill" Mattingly. Don attended Reitz Memorial High School — a Roman Catholic school in the Diocese of Evansville. Evansville-area Mattinglys trace to the Kentucky Catholic migration corridor: Marion County (Saint Mary), Kentucky, settled by Leonard Mattingly Jr. (1764–1843) and his sons. That is Hunter's confirmed patrilineal ancestor. The specific generational bridge from Marion County to Evansville has not been documented in accessible free databases, but the Catholic Indiana corridor is well established for this surname.

**Relation to Hunter:** Probable 4th–6th cousin. Both carry Catholic Kentucky Mattingly heritage tracing to the same Maryland colony source. Confidence: **0.55 (PROBABLE, circumstantial).**

### 2. Mack Mattingly (1931–present)
**Claim:** U.S. Senator from Georgia, 1981–1987. First Republican elected by popular vote to the U.S. Senate from Georgia since Reconstruction. Defeated Democratic incumbent Herman Talmadge in a major 1980 upset. Later U.S. Ambassador to the Seychelles (1992–1993) under Presidents George H.W. Bush and Bill Clinton. Air Force veteran; IBM executive.

**Lineage assessment:** Born Anderson, Indiana. Full name: Mack Francis Mattingly. Indiana birth places him in the same Catholic Kentucky-to-Indiana Mattingly migration corridor as Don Mattingly, though Anderson is in central Indiana rather than Evansville. Specific lineage not documented in accessible sources.

**Relation to Hunter:** Possible distant cousin. Confidence: **0.40 (LOW-PROBABLE).**

---

### 3. Marie Mattingly Meloney (1878–1943) — STRONGEST CONNECTION
**Claim:** One of the leading American women journalists of her era. Editor, New York Herald Tribune Sunday Magazine. In 1921 she personally solicited Marie Curie and organized a nationwide fund drive that raised $100,000 to purchase a gram of radium for Curie's laboratory in Paris — an act that made international headlines. Confidante of Eleanor Roosevelt in the 1930s. Her Time magazine obituary called her "fine lace made of cable wire."

**Lineage assessment:** Born **Bardstown, Kentucky** — the single most important geographic marker for this surname. Bardstown is the seat of the Catholic Mattingly settlement, home to the "Holy Land of Kentucky" Catholic community descended from the 1785–1790 Maryland migration. Her father was **Cyprian Peter Mattingly**, a physician, and her brother was **Judge Carroll Mattingly** (Georgetown University). The Bardstown Mattinglys are documented descendants of Leonard Mattingly Jr. (1764–1843), Marion County — Hunter's confirmed patrilineal ancestor via the Texas line. Marie was born approximately 30 years before Hunter's great-grandfather Leroy Mattingly (b. 1896), in the same Catholic community.

**Relation to Hunter:** Probable 4th–6th cousin (2–3 generations removed). Her father Cyprian and Hunter's Kentucky Mattingly progenitors almost certainly share a common grandfather or great-grandfather born in the 1790s–1810s. Confidence: **0.72 (HIGH-PROBABLE).**

### 4. Garrett Mattingly (1900–1962)
**Claim:** Pulitzer Prize-winning historian (Special Citation, 1960) for "The Armada" — a bestselling narrative history of the 1588 Spanish Armada. Also wrote "Catherine of Aragon" (1941) and "Renaissance Diplomacy" (1955). Columbia University professor; four-time Guggenheim Fellow; member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.

**Lineage assessment:** Born Washington, D.C. Family moved to Kalamazoo, Michigan in 1913. No parental names documented in Wikipedia. A Washington D.C. origin does not strongly contradict Maryland Catholic Mattingly descent — Maryland families commonly migrated to Washington. No positive lineage documentation available.

**Relation to Hunter:** Speculative. Confidence: **0.30 (SPECULATIVE).**

### 5. Ken Mattingly (1936–2023)
**Claim:** NASA astronaut; orbited the Moon on Apollo 16 (1972); flew STS-4 (1982) and STS-51-C (1985). Famously removed from Apollo 13 three days before launch due to measles exposure, then spent six days helping mission control engineer the rescue from the ground. Rear Admiral, U.S. Navy. One of only 24 humans to have reached the Moon.

**Lineage assessment:** Born Chicago, Illinois. Father: Thomas Kenneth Mattingly. Mother: Constance Mason (Clarke) Mattingly. Family relocated to Hialeah, Florida. Chicago/Illinois birth does not point strongly to the Kentucky Catholic corridor. No further lineage documented.

**Relation to Hunter:** Possible distant cousin — Mattingly is rare enough that most American bearers share common Maryland Catholic descent, but specific connection is unconfirmed. Confidence: **0.30 (LOW).**

### 6. Ignatius G. Mattingly (1927–2004)
**Claim:** Linguist and speech scientist. NSA analyst 1955–1966. Professor of Linguistics, University of Connecticut 1966–1996. Researcher, Haskins Laboratories at Yale 1966–2004. Co-developed (with Alvin Liberman) the Motor Theory of Speech Perception. Wrote the first computer program for synthesizing continuous speech from phonetic input (1964) — a foundational act in computational linguistics and assistive technology.

**Lineage assessment:** No birthplace given in Wikipedia. The given name "Ignatius" is the signature Catholic saint name of the Maryland Catholic Mattingly tradition — St. Inigoes (Ignatius) parish in St. Mary's County, Maryland, was the Catholic anchor of the Mattingly community. Hunter's direct ancestor Ignatius Mattingly (1704–1789, WikiTree Mattingly-60) carried this name. The name persisted through the Kentucky generations. Its appearance in a 20th-century Mattingly is a strong genealogical signal.

**Relation to Hunter:** Probable distant cousin. The "Ignatius" given name in this context is near-diagnostic of Maryland Catholic Mattingly descent. Confidence: **0.60 (PROBABLE).**

### 7. Ben Mattingly (distiller, fl. 1870s–1902)
**Claim:** Kentucky bourbon distillery owner. Saint Mary, Marion County, Kentucky. His distillery supplied whiskey to George Garvin Brown for the original Old Forester bourbon — the first bourbon sold exclusively in sealed glass bottles, now recognized as a historic American spirits brand. Brown purchased Mattingly's distillery outright in 1902. The separate Mattingly & Moore Distillery (Bardstown-area) trained Lambert Willett, who later founded Willett Distillery.

**Lineage assessment:** Saint Mary, Marion County, Kentucky is the **exact settlement hub** of the Catholic Mattinglys who descend from Leonard Mattingly Jr. (1764–1843). The entire Saint Mary Catholic community is composed of Leonard Jr. descendants. Ben Mattingly operating a distillery in Saint Mary is powerful evidence he is a direct male-line descendant of Leonard Jr. — Hunter's confirmed patrilineal ancestor.

**Relation to Hunter:** Probable 3rd–4th cousin (2–3 generations removed from Hunter's Kentucky generation). Confidence: **0.75 (HIGH-PROBABLE).**

---

### 8. Harold Mattingly (1884–1964) — English branch
**Claim:** British classical scholar. Assistant Keeper of Coins and Medals, British Museum 1910–1948. Authority on ancient Roman coinage and Tacitus. Father of Harold B. Mattingly (also a historian); grandfather of David Mattingly (archaeologist). A three-generation scholarly dynasty.

**Lineage note:** Born Sudbury, Suffolk, England. This is English Mattingley/Mattingly stock that did not emigrate. He shares the common English ancestor (Mattingley village, Hampshire) but is a collateral English cousin, not a descendant of the Maryland colonial migration. No meaningful genealogical proximity to Hunter in American terms.

## Section 2: Teichmueller Notables

### 9. Gustav Teichmuller (1832–1888) — MOST SIGNIFICANT FINDING
**Claim:** Major German philosopher. Professor at University of Basel (from 1868) and Imperial University of Dorpat/Tartu (from 1871). His concept of perspectivism directly influenced Friedrich Nietzsche's philosophy. Founded the Russian personalism school. Mentored Rudolf Christoph Eucken, who won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1908.

**Lineage assessment — PROBABLE SIBLING OF HANS TEICHMUELLER:**

The Wikipedia article on Gustav states: born Braunschweig, father **August Teichmueller**, lieutenant in the Prussian army, mother **Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth, nee von Girsewaldt**.

The 1902 Fayette County, Texas history (F. Lotto, LOC) states that Hans Teichmueller (1837–1901) — Hunter's maternal great-great-grandfather's father-in-law — was born in Braunschweig, and his parents were **August Teichmueller**, an officer in the Brunswick army, and **Charlotte Teichmueller, nee von Gursewald**.

The alignment is exact: same father's first name, same father's military rank in the Duchy of Brunswick, same mother's maiden name (von Girsewaldt / von Gursewald — variant spellings of the same noble surname), same city. Gustav was born in 1832 and Hans in 1837, five years apart in the same Braunschweig household. They are almost certainly brothers.

**Relation to Hunter:** Probable great-great-great-granduncle (via Teichmueller maternal line). The generational chain: Hunter → Shari (grandmother) → Leroy Mattingly (b. 1896) → Mary "May" Teichmueller Mattingly (d. 1900) → Hans Teichmueller (1837–1901) → [Hans's brother] Gustav Teichmueller (1832–1888). Confidence: **0.82 (HIGH-PROBABLE).**

### 10. Anna Teichmuller, composer (1861–1940)
**Claim:** German composer and teacher. Set poems of Carl Hauptmann and other poets to music. Worked at the Schreiberhau artist colony in Silesia. Published through Hildegard Publishing (contemporary women's music press).

**Lineage assessment:** Born Gottingen, daughter of Gustav Teichmueller and Anna von Cramer. If Gustav and Hans are brothers (confidence 0.82), Anna is Hans's niece and therefore Hunter's ancestor May Teichmueller Mattingly's first cousin.

**Relation to Hunter:** Probable first cousin twice removed of Hunter's ancestor May Teichmueller Mattingly. Confidence: **0.72 (PROBABLE, contingent on Gustav-Hans sibling confirmation).**

---

### 11. Minette Teichmueller Pohl (1871–1970) — CONFIRMED
**Claim:** American muralist. Painted "The Law, Texas Rangers" (1940) for the Smithville, Texas post office — a WPA/Treasury Section commission. Trained at Chicago Academy of Fine Arts and San Antonio Academy of Art. Married artist Hugo Pohl. Lived 99 years.

**Lineage — CONFIRMED:** Born La Grange, Texas, daughter of Hans Teichmueller (1837–1901). She is the **sister** of Mary "May" Teichmueller Mattingly, who married Ed Mattingly (assistant cashier, First National Bank, La Grange). May was Hunter's ancestor Leroy Mattingly's grandmother. Therefore Minette is Leroy's great-aunt and Hunter's confirmed great-great-grandaunt via the Teichmueller-Mattingly marriage.

**Relation to Hunter:** CONFIRMED great-great-grandaunt. Confidence: **0.97.**

---

### 12. Robert Teichmuller (1863–1939)
**Claim:** German concert pianist and music educator. Professor, Leipzig Conservatory from 1908. One of the most influential piano teachers of his era. Students included Eileen Joyce, Gunther Ramin, and Ernst Oster.

**Lineage assessment:** Born Braunschweig 1863. The same city as Gustav (b. 1832) and Hans (b. 1837) Teichmueller. He could be a younger sibling of Hans, a nephew of Hans via Gustav, or from a separate Braunschweig Teichmueller family. Insufficient genealogical data to assign specific relationship.

**Relation to Hunter:** Possible collateral Teichmueller relative. Confidence: **0.30 (LOW-POSSIBLE).**

## Key Takeaways

1. **Gustav Teichmuller** (influenced Nietzsche) is almost certainly Hunter's great-great-great-granduncle. This is the single most historically significant probable relative identified.

2. **Minette Teichmueller Pohl** (WPA muralist) is Hunter's **confirmed** great-great-grandaunt. She has a Wikipedia article and WikiTree profile.

3. **Marie Mattingly Meloney** (organized Marie Curie's radium fund) was born in Bardstown, the Mattingly clan seat, and is almost certainly Hunter's 4th–6th cousin (removed).

4. **Ben Mattingly** (distiller in Saint Mary, Marion County, KY — Old Forester bourbon supply) was almost certainly a 3rd–4th cousin of Hunter's Kentucky ancestors.

5. **Don Mattingly** (Yankees) and **Mack Mattingly** (U.S. Senator) both come from Indiana, which draws from the same Catholic Kentucky Mattingly migration corridor, making them probable distant cousins of Hunter.

6. **Ignatius G. Mattingly** (linguist, speech synthesis pioneer) carries the "Ignatius" given name that is diagnostic of Maryland Catholic Mattingly descent and is probably a distant cousin.

## Open Questions

- **Gustav Teichmuller sibling confirmation:** The 1832 Braunschweig parish register (Kirchenbuch) at the Niedersächsisches Landesarchiv, Wolfenbüttel branch, would confirm or deny the Gustav-Hans sibling bond. This is a one-letter request to that archive.
- **Cyprian Peter Mattingly's parents:** A FamilySearch search for Cyprian Mattingly (physician, Bardstown KY, born ~1835–1850) would identify which son of Leonard Mattingly Jr. he descends from, giving Marie Mattingly Meloney a precise cousin degree.
- **Don Mattingly's Bill Mattingly's parents:** Evansville newspaper death notices for William "Bill" Mattingly would give parents' names and birth state, confirming the Kentucky connection.
- **Ignatius G. Mattingly birthplace:** NSA employment records (unlikely) or Yale alumni records (possible) might give birthplace and parents.
