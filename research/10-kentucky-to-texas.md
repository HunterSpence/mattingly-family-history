# Kentucky to Texas: Filling the Mattingly Gap (1800–1870)

**Compiled:** 2026-04-25  
**Scope:** Bridge the generational gap between Leonard Mattingly Jr. (d.1843, Marion County, Kentucky) and Ed Mattingly (alive 1934, La Grange, Fayette County, Texas). Approximately 3–4 generations must be identified.  
**Confidence tiers:** CONFIRMED (3+ independent primary sources) | PROBABLE (2 sources) | POSSIBLE (1 source, plausible) | UNVERIFIED (claim exists, no source)

---

## The Known Anchors

**Start:** Leonard Mattingly Jr., b. 1764 Maryland, d. 1843 Marion County, Kentucky. Catholic, Washington/Marion County KY community. Four confirmed children. WikiTree-210.

**End:** Ed Mattingly, assistant cashier of the First National Bank of La Grange, Fayette County, Texas. Married May Teichmueller (daughter of Judge Hans Teichmueller). Children: Leroy Teichmuller Mattingly (b.1898), Claude Mattingly (~b.1899, d.January 31 1934), Mamie Mattingly (~b.1900–1902). Confirmed alive in La Grange in January 1934 when son Claude died. Source: Frank Lotto, *Fayette County: Her History and Her People* (1902), LOC item 02023423.

**The gap:** Three to four generations, roughly 1820–1890, not yet confirmed in any accessible primary source.

**George Thomas Mattingly (b.1830, WikiTree-695) is the strongest Texas migration candidate.** He is the only grandchild of Leonard Jr. with no documented death record. His siblings all have confirmed Kentucky death records. At age 30 in 1860, he was at the prime age for westward migration. If he moved to Texas around 1860–1870 and fathered a son in the mid-1860s, that son could be Ed Mattingly.

Confidence for George Thomas as Texas migrant: **POSSIBLE** — best available candidate; no confirmation in Texas sources found.

**Edward Mattingly (b.1840, WikiTree-701) is eliminated as Ed's father:** WikiTree confirms he died March 13, 1910 in Marion, Kentucky. He is not the same person as Ed Mattingly of La Grange (who was alive 1934), and he cannot be Ed's father if his death was in Kentucky with no Texas-connected children recorded.

## Generation 8: The Migration Bridge (born ~1830–1850)

This is the person who physically moved from Kentucky to Texas. The most likely scenario:

- Born ~1830–1850 in Kentucky (probably Marion or Washington County)
- Migrated to Texas ~1860–1880, likely drawn by post-Civil War land opportunity or Catholic community networks
- Arrived in Fayette County, Texas, where the German-Catholic community was established
- Had a son Ed Mattingly born in Texas ~1860s–1870s

This generation remains **UNVERIFIED**. It is a placeholder in the chain, not a confirmed person. It may or may not be George Thomas Mattingly himself — he could represent either Generation 7 (if he migrated young and Ed is his son) or could bridge Generation 8 if an intermediate generation exists.

## Generation 9: Ed Mattingly of La Grange, Texas (CONFIRMED)

Ed Mattingly is the best-documented person in the gap, confirmed from two independent sources:

**Occupation (1902):** Assistant cashier, First National Bank of La Grange. Source: Frank Lotto, *Fayette County: Her History and Her People* (Schulenburg, TX: Sticker Steam Press, 1902). This is the highest-confidence primary source for the Mattingly Texas presence.

**Alive 1934:** When son Claude Mattingly died by suicide in Austin on January 31, 1934, AP wire articles ran February 1–6, 1934 and referenced Ed in La Grange. Claude's body was returned there.

**Family:**
- Wife: May Teichmueller, daughter of Judge Hans Teichmueller (b.1837 Braunschweig Germany, d.Feb 17 1901 La Grange TX). Hans served as District Judge of the 22nd Judicial District 1884–1901. He had five children; Minette Teichmueller (b.Jan 27 1871, muralist, d.1970) is the only confirmed sibling of May in accessible sources.
- May died in childbirth delivering daughter Mamie (~1900–1902) per oral tradition.
- Son Leroy Teichmuller Mattingly (b.1898): Shari's grandfather. The "Teichmuller" middle name preserves May's maiden name.
- Son Claude Mattingly (~b.1899): pediatrician in Austin, UT Medical Branch Galveston graduate, Marine Corps WWI veteran.

**Birth year:** Not confirmed. Estimated 1860–1875 based on his children's birth years. The 1902 Lotto book may contain his age and birthplace — full text has not been accessed.

**Parentage:** **UNVERIFIED.** No source directly links Ed Mattingly to a Kentucky Mattingly ancestor.

Confidence: **CONFIRMED** for occupation, wife, children, and Texas presence. **UNVERIFIED** for birth year, birth place, and parents.

## The Centenarian Claim: Examined and Not Identified

Shari's oral tradition describes a Mattingly ancestor born 1828 who died in 1935 at age 107.

The only documented Mattingly with a matching birth year is **Leonard Mattingly III (b.Nov 15 1828, WikiTree-1836)**. He is definitively eliminated: WikiTree confirms he died September 5, 1914 in Glen Dean, Breckinridge County, **Kentucky**, at age 85. He did not reach Texas and did not live to 107.

No other Mattingly with a birth year near 1828 has been identified in any accessible source. The centenarian claim remains **UNVERIFIED** — it is oral tradition only, and the only matching documented person died in Kentucky 21 years before the claimed 1935 death.

If this person was real, a 107-year-old dying in Texas in 1935 would have attracted extensive newspaper coverage. The Briscoe Center for American History at UT Austin (lib-briscoe@utlists.utexas.edu) holds microfilm for Texas newspapers including the *La Grange Journal* and *San Antonio Express* from that period. A targeted search for a Mattingly obituary in 1935 is the most direct way to confirm or disprove this claim.

## Open Questions

1. **Who are Ed Mattingly's parents?** — The single most important unresolved question. Best candidates are descendants of George Thomas Mattingly (b.1830 Marion KY, WikiTree-695, no death record) or unrecorded children of Edward Mattingly (b.1840 Marion KY, WikiTree-701). The 1880 and 1900 Texas census and the 1902 Lotto book are the highest-priority sources.

2. **What does Frank Lotto's 1902 book say about Ed Mattingly?** — The book has 600+ individual biographies. Ed's entry may include birthplace, age, and parents. LOC item 02023423 is inaccessible online (Cloudflare block); the UT Press 1981 reprint is available at university libraries.

3. **Did George Thomas Mattingly (b.1830, WikiTree-695) move to Texas?** — He is the only documented grandchild of Leonard Jr. with no death record. The 1860, 1870, and 1880 Texas census for surname Mattingly would confirm or eliminate him.

4. **What is May Teichmueller's full name and birth year?** — Hans Teichmueller had five children. The 1880 US census for his household in La Grange (Fayette County, Texas) would list all children with ages.

5. **What was Ed Mattingly's birth year and birth place?** — His age in the 1902 book or his 1900 census entry would establish this.

6. **When and where did Ed Mattingly die?** — He was alive in January 1934. Texas death certificate records 1934–1960 for "Edward Mattingly" in Fayette County would close this.

7. **Who was the centenarian born 1828?** — If real, Texas newspapers 1928 (100th birthday) or 1935 (death at 107) would carry the story. Leonard III (b.1828) is confirmed to have died in Kentucky 1914 and is eliminated.
