## 2. The SPENCE Surname — Etymology and Origin

### Etymology (HIGH confidence — multiple concordant sources)

**Primary derivation:** Old French *despense*, from Latin *dispendere* ("to dispense / to weigh out"). The *de-* prefix was dropped in Middle English, leaving *spense* or *spence*, meaning "larder," "storeroom," or "pantry."

**Occupational meaning:** The surname was a metonymic occupational name for the person in charge of the *spence* — the food storage and dispensing room of a great house, monastery, or castle. This was a real position of trust and middle-rank authority in a medieval household, not a servant role. The *spenser* (or *despenser*) controlled food distribution to the household.

**Variant forms:**
- Spens, Spense, Spensely, Spenceley
- Spencer (the extended occupational form, meaning "one who works the spence")
- The Despenser family (Norman aristocracy) is the grand version of the same root

**First documentary record (HIGH confidence):** Simon del Spens (also written *de la Despense*) appears in the Charters of Guisburn Priory, Yorkshire, England, in 1300. Other 14th-century records include Amice ate Spense in the Subsidy Rolls of Essex (1327) and William atte Spence in the Subsidy Rolls of Worcester (1327).

### Geographic Origin — England and Scotland (HIGH confidence)

The Spence surname is classified with English and Scottish origins. While the first documented record is Yorkshire, the name established deep roots in Scotland, where it became particularly concentrated. By the 1881 census, *Spence* was among the recognizable surnames of Lowland Scotland (Borders region, Fife, Lothian).

**Global distribution today (Forebears.io data, ~2014):**
- Approximately 100,165 people bear the surname worldwide
- Ranked 5,643rd globally (1 in ~72,755 people)
- Most prevalent country: **United States** (48,186 individuals, ranked 894th)
- Highest density country: **Jamaica** (1 in 350 people — very high; reflects colonial-era Scottish plantation settlement)
- England: 14,198 | Canada: 10,528 | Australia: 6,417 | Scotland: 4,663

**Within the United States** (Name Census data): approximately 37,305 Spences, ranked 808th most common surname. The surname is most concentrated in the South and mid-Atlantic states, consistent with the Scottish-Irish migration corridor through Virginia → Tennessee → Kentucky → Georgia/Alabama/Mississippi → Texas.

Monroe's paternal great-great-grandfather Patrick Andrew Monroe emigrated from Scotland in the mid-17th century. The "great-grandfather Patrick Spence" referenced above was Monroe's maternal ancestor (his mother's grandfather), explaining why the Spence name entered the Monroe family as a given name for Monroe's father.

**Significance for Hunter:** If the Spence family that produced Hunter's grandfather shares any common ancestry with the Virginia Spences who became the maternal line of James Monroe, Hunter would have a very distant connection to the fifth President of the United States. ## 4. Probable Migration Path: Spence Surname to Texas

The documented migration corridor for families bearing the Spence surname follows a well-established Scottish-Scots-Irish pattern:

**England/Scotland (pre-1700) → Virginia/Maryland (1620s-1750s) → Appalachian interior (1750s-1800s) → Tennessee/Kentucky (early 1800s) → Deep South / Gulf States (1820s-1850s) → Texas (1840s-1900s)**

The Texas State Historical Association confirms that Scots immigrants to Texas came "singly or in small groups, some as early as 1825," dispersing rapidly into the general population. No specific Spence colony was established, but individual Spence families would have arrived in this wave. The 1880 and 1900 US Census records for Texas show Spence families present across Bexar County (San Antonio), Travis County (Austin), and the Hill Country.

**Assessment:** A David Spence born in Texas around 1945-1948 would be the third or fourth generation of a Texas Spence line, with family roots in the Deep South or Appalachian states going back to the early 19th century, and ultimately to Virginia colonial settlers of the 17th century. This is a strong and historically consistent pattern.

## 5. Notable Figures Named Spence

These are documented notable individuals sharing Hunter's paternal surname. Direct family relationship to David Spence's specific lineage is **unverified** for all of them.

### Historical Figures

**Thomas Spence (1750-1814)** — English radical land reformer, born Newcastle upon Tyne to a Presbyterian family. Led the London Corresponding Society, imprisoned seven months in Newgate Gaol (1794) on a charge of high treason, sentenced to 12 months for seditious libel (1801). Died in poverty but authored *Spence's Plan*, an early land nationalization proposal that influenced later agrarian reform movements. Wikipedia-verified.

**Patrick Spence (date unknown, 17th century)** — Virginia settler, documented great-grandfather of President James Monroe (via Monroe's mother's Spence ancestry). HouseOfNames citation, partially corroborated by Monroe's Wikipedia article.

**William Spence (1747-1821)** — United Empire Loyalist, settled in Hampton, Kings County, New Brunswick, Canada, c. 1784. Chose to leave the newly formed United States rather than give up British loyalty.

### Modern Notable Spences (Wikipedia-verified)

**Gerry Spence (1929-2025)** — American trial lawyer, Laramie, Wyoming. Famous for never losing a criminal defense case in 40+ years of practice. Defended Karen Silkwood's estate; acquitted Imelda Marcos; defended Randy Weaver (Ruby Ridge). University of Wyoming Law School, class of 1952, graduated first in his class.

**Basil Spence (1907-1976)** — British architect, born Bombay (British India), son of Urwin Archibald Spence (assayer with the Royal Mint). Educated at George Watson's College, Edinburgh; Edinburgh College of Art. Won the competition to design **Coventry Cathedral** (rebuilt after WWII bombing), completed 1962 — one of the most significant modern buildings in Britain. Knighted in 1960.

**Brent Spence (1874-1967)** — American congressman from Kentucky (5th District), served 1931-1963. Born Newport, Kentucky, to Philip and Virginia (Berry) Spence. The **Brent Spence Bridge** over the Ohio River at Cincinnati is named for him.

**Errol Spence Jr. (born 1990)** — American professional boxer, former unified WBA/WBC/IBF welterweight champion. Born Long Island, New York; trained and based in DeSoto, Texas. (Confirms Spence is an active surname in modern Texas sporting life, though Errol's family origins are different from a Scottish-lineage Texas Spence family.)

**Catherine Helen Spence (1825-1910)** — Australian novelist, journalist, and women's suffrage leader. Born Melrose, Scotland; emigrated to South Australia 1839. First woman to stand for political office in Australia (1897).

**Skip Spence (1946-1999)** — Canadian-born American rock musician. Co-founder of Moby Grape; drummer on Jefferson Airplane's debut album. Born Alexander Lee Spence Jr., Windsor, Ontario.

**Errol Spence Sr. lineage** (Texas) — separate from the boxer above: The Spence name has been established in Texas African-American communities since Reconstruction, representing a different migration and naming pattern.
