## Critical Finding: Line Mismatch

This book does NOT cover Hunter's direct Mattingly line (Ignatius 1704 → Leonard Sr. 1739 → KY). It covers HENRY MATTINGLY I (c.1750–1823), an Allegany County, Maryland progenitor of Western Maryland, Ohio, Indiana, and Illinois families. The two lines are contemporaneous but distinct.

**Relationship between the two lines:**
- The book's introduction (p. iii) records: "Another tradition indicates that Henry was the brother of three Mattinglys, Leonard, William and Luke, who left Leonardtown, Md., and went to Kentucky about 1791." (OCR line 104)
- Charles Mattingly (1803–1897) testified that Henry I "was very much hurt when he heard of his brother going to Kentucky without stopping to see him on his way." (p. iii, OCR line 105)
- Appendix II (Hoye, p. 230) states: "The Kentucky Mattinglys trace their ancestry to Charles, whose sons, William, Leonard and Luke, settled in Hardin Creek, Kentucky, in 1786." (OCR lines 14115–14116)

**Implication:** The Leonard who went to Kentucky (Hunter's Generation 4 ancestor) may be a brother of Henry I — placing both lines as children of a John Mattingly (~1720, St. Mary's Co.). The post-publication letter (OCR lines 21773–21780) confirms Henry I was born July 3, 1751, the sixth child of John and Elizabeth Mattingly; the oldest child was born 1741, meaning John (~1720) was the father. This is a probable but unconfirmed bridge to Hunter's Ignatius 1704 → Leonard line.

## Henry I: Confirmed Biographical Facts

**Henry Mattingly I** (b. July 3, 1751, St. Mary's Co., Md. — d. June 21, 1823, Cumberland/Allegany Co., Md.)
- Corrected birth date from post-publication letter (p. iii says "about 1750"; letter says July 3, 1751). (OCR lines 21773–21780)
- Son of **John Mattingly** (~1720) and **Elizabeth Mattingly** (maiden name unknown); sixth of at least six children. (OCR line 21773)
- Married **Honor Durbin** (date unknown). (p. iv, Appendix II p. 230)
- 1778: Took Oath of Fidelity to the Revolution, Washington Co., Md., alongside Barnet, Joseph, and Richard Mattingly — "presumably sons of Luke Mattingly who resided near Hagerstown, Md." (p. iii, v; Appendix II p. 230)
- 1787: Allotted military lots 3666 and 3667 as an early settler near Mt. Savage, Allegany Co. (Appendix II p. 230)
- 1788: Listed among settlers west of Fort Cumberland. (*J. Thomas Scharf, History of Western Maryland*, vol. II, p. 1343; quoted p. v)
- 1800: Census, Wills Town, Allegany Co. (p. v)
- 1813: Property assessment, Cumberland, Md., $65.00. (*History of Cumberland, Md.*, Lowdermilk, p. 293; quoted p. v)
- Will probated June 21, 1823, Allegany Co. Court House, Cumberland, Md. (Appendix I, pp. 227–229)

**Will of Henry I (April 14, 1823; probated June 21, 1823):** Names four sons (John, Samuel, Henry, William) and eight daughters (Elizabeth Preston, Sarah McKenzie, Lucy, Mary, Honor, Hannah, Ann Logsdon, Jane Logsdon). Leaves $1 to daughter Jane Logsdon and $20 to Rev. Timothy Ryan. Witnesses: William Ridgely, Jona Arnold, Raphael Logsdon. (Appendix I, pp. 227–229)

**In the U.S. Census of 1790:** Fourteen Mattingly families listed in St. Mary's County. (Appendix II, p. 230)

## Connection to Hunter's Line: Key Assessment

The book explicitly identifies that Henry I's brothers — or near-kin — included **Leonard** and **Luke** who went to Kentucky (~1791). Hunter's direct ancestor **Leonard Mattingly Sr.** (1739–1829) migrated to Kentucky. The book's Henry I (b. 1751) would be a roughly contemporary candidate for a brother or first cousin of Leonard Sr. (b. 1739), both from St. Mary's Co., Md.

The post-publication letter (OCR lines 21773–21780) pushes Henry I's lineage back to:
- **Henry I** (b. July 3, 1751) — sixth child of John (~1720) and Elizabeth
- This **John (~1720)** could be a son of Hunter's **Ignatius (~1704)**, making Henry I and Leonard Sr. potential first cousins rather than brothers.

This remains unconfirmed in the book itself — the compilers note the colonial-period research was incomplete.

## Hoye Appendix: Garrett County, Maryland Line

The Hoye material (Appendix II, published May 7, 1936 in Mountain Democrat) covers the Dominick Mattingly (1808–1854) branch at Hoyes/Johnstown, Garrett Co., Md.:
- Dominick married **Anne Browning** (daughter of hunter Meshack Browning), March 1, 1832. (Appendix II p. 232)
- Dominick's children: Mary (1833), Anne Celestine (1834), Meshack (1837–1912), William H. (1840–1863), Elizabeth E. (1843–1871). (Appendix II p. 233)
- Meshack Mattingly (1837–1912) married **Mary P. Griffith** (daughter of Gov. George W. Griffith of Pennsylvania); built Hoyes into a prominent stock farm. (Appendix II p. 233)
- St. Dominick Church (renamed from St. James, built 1853; rebuilt 1890) at Hoyes, Md., is named for Dominick Mattingly. (Appendix II p. 234)
- Rev. Romanus Mattingly (1850–1922), son of Sylvester (No. 18), built the Oakland and Hoyes churches; died at St. Agnes Hospital, Baltimore, survived by brother Alexander. (Appendix II pp. 234–235)

## Historical Context: Catholic Migration Patterns

- Maryland as Catholic haven: The Mattingly family among earliest St. Mary's Co. Catholic settlers (~1663). The book notes "in 1947, prior to establishment of Patuxent Naval Air Base, there were 11,036 Catholics in a total population of 14,626" in St. Mary's Co. (p. ii)
- The "Holy Land of Kentucky": Not explicitly named in this book, but Hardin Creek / Marion County, Kentucky mentioned as the Kentucky Mattingly homeland. (Appendix II p. 230)
- Blooming Rose, Garrett Co., Md.: A documented Catholic settlement where James Mattingly and brother John lived in the 1820s–1830s. (p. 41; Hoye p. 231)
- Mattingly Settlement, Muskingum Co., Ohio: Founded ~1812 by William Mattingly (No. 40); became center of Ohio Catholic Mattingly families; St. Mary's Church built 1856. (p. 196)
