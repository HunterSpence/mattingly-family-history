# Kellner / Mollenhauer / Girsewald Deep Follow-Up

**Research date:** 2026-04-26  
**Scope:** Continue from `45-teichmueller-kellner-expanded.md` and revisit the open questions around Augusta/Auguste Kellner, muralist Minette Teichmueller, the Mollenhauer branch, and whether the Girsewald/Boyer line can be pushed one or two generations further using accessible public sources only.

---

## Bottom Line

1. **Augusta / Auguste Kellner is still only partly resolved.** She is securely documented as Hans Teichmueller's wife, and her father is described by TSHA as a modern-language teacher, but I did **not** find an accessible public source in this pass that names that father or Augusta's mother.[1][2]
2. **The muralist Minette Teichmueller is confirmed as the Smithville post-office painter, but the direct parent-child proof to Hans + Augusta is still not fully closed from institutional records.** The best public evidence remains a secondary Texas-art biography that calls her their daughter; I am treating that as **probable**, not closed proof.[3][4]
3. **The Mollenhauer branch is confirmed at the marriage-generation level only.** Deutsche Biographie securely identifies Hans's sister Wilhelmina (Minette) Teichmüller as wife of Karl Mollenhauer, and Bockenem's church lexicon confirms Karl Johann Mollenhauer in the Bockenem superintendent/pastor role, but I did not find a responsibly citable descendant chain from accessible public sources in this pass.[2][5]
4. **The Girsewald/Boyer line is still safest at one more confirmed generation only:** Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth's parents **Georg von Girsewald** and **Christine Elisabeth Boyer**. I found an institutional archival pointer to a **"Stammtafel der Familie Girsewald aus Dassel,"** but not viewable content strong enough to name Georg's parents or Boyer's parents from the public page alone.[2][6][7]

## Direct Ancestors and Spouse Line

### 1. Augusta / Auguste Kellner

**Confidence:**  
- **CONFIRMED** as Hans Teichmueller's wife  
- **PARTIAL / UNRESOLVED** for her own parentage

**Exact source statements**

- TSHA states that Hans "married Augusta Kellner, whose father, a modern-language teacher" had taught him English, and that "The Teichmuellers had five children."[1]
- Deutsche Biographie's 2016 NDB genealogy names Hans as "Hans (1837-1901, ⚭ Auguste Kellner)."[2]
- The UNT Portal metadata for the 1908 memorial volume identifies the creator as **Auguste Kellner** and says the book was "Prepared by his loving wife and daughters."[4]

**Assessment**

This is enough to keep **Augusta / Auguste Kellner** as a secure spouse and mother in the Texas branch. It is **not** enough to name her father or mother. In this pass I did not locate an accessible public death certificate, marriage register, census entry, church register, or county-history page image that identifies the teacher by name.

So the correct status remains:

- **Augusta Kellner = confirmed spouse**
- **Augusta Kellner's father = confirmed only as an unnamed modern-language teacher**
- **Augusta Kellner's mother = unresolved**

---

### 2. Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth (von Girsewald) and her parents

**Confidence:** **CONFIRMED**

**Exact source statements**

- NDB gives: "M Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth (1799-1860), T d. Georg v. Girsewald (1735-1816) ... u. d. Christine Elisabeth Boyer."[2]
- The separate Deutsche Biographie index entry for **Georg von Girsewald** gives **1735-1816** and identifies him as **Oberstallmeister**, **Hauptmann**, and **Offizier**.[7]

**Assessment**

This is the strongest clean result from this follow-up. The maternal Teichmüller line can still be stated confidently as:

1. **Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth von Girsewald** (1799-1860)  
2. daughter of **Georg von Girsewald** (1735-1816)  
3. and **Christine Elisabeth Boyer**

That is a real, source-backed extension from the Texas line into the Girsewald/Boyer generation.

---

### 3. Can the Girsewald / Boyer line be pushed another generation?

**Confidence:** **NOT RESPONSIBLY EXTENDED IN THIS PASS**

**Accessible public evidence found**

- Deutsche Digitale Bibliothek exposes an archival item under **Urkunden der Familie von Dassel** that includes **"Stammtafel der Familie Girsewald aus Dassel."**[6]

**Assessment**

This is a useful archival lead, but not a usable genealogical proof in its current public presentation. The public DDB page shows that a family table exists in an institutional archive; it does **not** expose the contents clearly enough to identify Georg's parents or Christine Elisabeth Boyer's parents.

So the responsible stopping point is:

- **Confirmed:** Georg von Girsewald + Christine Elisabeth Boyer  
- **Not yet responsibly extendable from accessible public content:** the next Girsewald or Boyer generation

## Collateral and Notable Branches

### 4. Wilhelmina (Minette) Teichmüller / Karl Mollenhauer

**Confidence:**  
- **CONFIRMED** for the couple and Karl's office  
- **UNRESOLVED** for descendants

**Exact source statements**

- NDB identifies Hans's sister as "Wilhelmina (Minette) (1829-86, ⚭ Karl Mollenhauer, 1818-64)."[2]
- The same NDB genealogy describes Karl as "Pastor, Sup. in Bockenem, Ambergau."[2]
- The Bockenem church lexicon lists **Karl Johann Mollenhauer** on the first pastorate / superintendent line for **1860-1867**.[5]

**Assessment**

This is enough to confirm a real Mollenhauer branch attached to Hunter's Teichmüller line:

- **Wilhelmina (Minette) Teichmüller**  
- wife of **Karl Johann Mollenhauer**  
- who served at **Bockenem**

What I did **not** find in accessible public sources this pass was a descendant list tied clearly enough to this couple to name later Mollenhauer children or grandchildren without falling back on unsourced trees.

So:

- **Marriage-generation Mollenhauer branch: confirmed**
- **Mollenhauer descendants: not added**

---

### 5. Muralist Minette Teichmueller

**Confidence:**  
- **CONFIRMED** as the Smithville muralist  
- **PROBABLE, not closed** as daughter of Hans + Augusta

**Exact source statements**

- The New Deal Art Registry identifies the Smithville work as **"The Law--Texas Rangers"**, dated **1940**, medium **oil on canvas**, by **Minette Teichmueller** under the **Treasury Section of Fine Arts**.[3]
- A Texas art-market biography states that she was "the daughter of Judge Hans Teichmueller and Augusta Kellner."[8]
- The same biography also says she later lived with her niece **Mayme Mattingly Martin**.[8]
- TSHA independently says Hans and Augusta had **five children**.[1]
- The UNT memorial metadata says the 1908 family volume was prepared by Hans's wife and daughters.[4]

**Assessment**

The public record set currently supports two different confidence levels:

**Closed / confirmed**
- A real Texas artist named **Minette Teichmueller** painted the Smithville federal mural.[3]

**Still not fully closed**
- The direct parent-child statement tying that artist to **Hans Teichmueller + Augusta Kellner** is still resting mainly on a **secondary commercial biography**, not on a census, death certificate, probate file, cemetery inscription, or church/vital record image that I could cite directly in this pass.[8]

That means the right wording is still:

- **Minette Teichmueller the muralist = confirmed**
- **Minette Teichmueller as daughter of Hans + Augusta = probable, not yet institutionally closed**

## What I Would Treat as Integration-Ready vs. Not Yet Ready

### Integration-ready from this pass

1. **Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth Teichmüller née von Girsewald** was daughter of **Georg von Girsewald** and **Christine Elisabeth Boyer**.[2][7]
2. **Wilhelmina (Minette) Teichmüller (1829-1886)** was sister of Hans and wife of **Karl Mollenhauer (1818-1864)**.[2]
3. **Karl Mollenhauer** held the Bockenem superintendent/pastor post documented in the Bockenem church lexicon.[5]

### Not yet ready

1. Naming **Augusta Kellner's father** or mother
2. Treating muralist **Minette Teichmueller** as conclusively proven daughter of Hans + Augusta
3. Adding any **Mollenhauer descendants**
4. Extending **Girsewald** or **Boyer** one more generation
