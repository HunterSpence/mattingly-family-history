## Branch 1: Teichmueller (Leroy's Maternal Grandfather)

### Hans's German parents — CONFIRMED (primary source: 1902 Fayette County history, LOC)

The 1902 county history states explicitly:

> "His parents were August and Charlotte Teichmueller, nee von Gursewald. His father was an officer in the Brunswick army."

**August Teichmueller** (Hans's father): Officer in the Duchy of Brunswick army. His death left the family in financial difficulty, prompting Hans to emigrate to the United States in 1856. No birth or death year found in accessible sources.

**Charlotte Teichmueller (born von Gursewald)** (Hans's mother): Her maiden name "von Gursewald" carries the aristocratic "von" prefix, indicating a minor noble or gentry family of the Duchy of Brunswick. No separate Wikipedia or genealogical record for this family was found in accessible online sources; the 1902 Fayette County history is the sole surviving citation.

Hans attended the public schools of Brunswick city and the **Gymnasium at Blankenburg (Harz)** — a town in what is now Saxony-Anhalt, Germany, about 40 km southeast of Brunswick. The current Gymnasium Am Thie in Blankenburg is the successor to the school Hans attended. He graduated, then worked briefly as a ship's sailor to New York as a teenager, returned home, and emigrated permanently to Texas in 1856 after his father's death.

### Hans's five children — CONFIRMED

The 1902 history names the surviving children at the time of writing (Hans died February 17, 1901):
- **May Mattingly** (died approximately 1900) — wife of Ed. Mattingly, assistant cashier of the First National Bank of La Grange
- **Minetta Teichmueller** — unmarried, living with her mother in La Grange in 1902
- **Anna Teichmueller** — unmarried, living with her mother in La Grange in 1902
- Two additional children (unnamed in the biography; names lost)

### Augusta Kellner (Hans's wife) — CONFIRMED

**Augusta Kellner Teichmueller** was born at Postoak Point, Austin County, Texas, daughter of "a highly educated teacher of modern languages." She married Hans on June 16, 1858 at Postoak Point; Colonel Henderson, justice of the peace, performed the ceremony. She was a widow and head of household in La Grange in 1902.

## Branch 2: Mattingly Paternal — English Origins and Colonial Line

### The English village — CONFIRMED (Wikipedia)

The Mattingly surname originates in **Mattingley**, a village and civil parish in **Hampshire, England**, located on the Reading road between Hook and Reading. The River Whitewater runs through the parish. Wikipedia states directly: "The Mattingly surname originates in Mattingley." The name comes from "Matta's ley" — "ley" meaning place in Old English, referring to the Matta family. This is 40 miles southwest of London.

### Thomas Mattingly I — CONFIRMED (WikiTree Mattingly-30; jhink.org; Herman Mattingly 1975)

Thomas Mattingly I (abt. 1624 - abt. 24 July 1664) was the family's American immigrant. Born in Sussex/Berkshire England (WikiTree shows "Omny Parish, Sussex" — likely a transcription of a specific hamlet), he emigrated with wife and four children in 1663-64. He died intestate shortly after arrival in St. Mary's County, Maryland. Widow Elizabeth McWilliams was granted administration; she remarried attorney Walter Pake, who secured the family's land claim. The 300-acre "Mattingly's Hope" patent in Charles County, Maryland was issued 4 September 1666 to sons Thomas II and Cezar.

**Thomas I's English ancestry (WikiTree):**
- Father: **Charles Mattingly** (abt. 1600-1642, Omny Parish, Sussex)
- Mother: **Judith Bugbee** (abt. 1602, England)
- Paternal grandfather: **John Mattingly** (abt. 1560-1641, Berkshire, England)
- Paternal great-grandfather: **Francis Mattingly** (1580-1620, England)

Thomas I's wife: **Elizabeth McWilliams** (abt. 1626 - after 1669, England).

Thomas I's four children:
- **Thomas Mattingly II** (abt. 1650-1715) — the heir line
- **Cezar (Caesar) Mattingly** (1654-1719) — co-heir of Mattingly's Hope
- **Judith Mattingly** (1645-1744) — married, became Judith Turner; lived to ~99
- **Elizabeth Dorcas Mattingly** (1656-1749) — lived to ~93

### Thomas Mattingly II — CONFIRMED (WikiTree Mattingly-29; FamilySearch)

Thomas II (abt. 1650-1715) married twice. First wife: Mary Elizabeth Suttle/Curtis (about 1675, St. Mary's County); children Elizabeth (1681) and Judith (1683). Second wife: Elizabeth Cole (about 1690); children Thomas III (1688/1690), James (1696), Charles (1698), William (1700), Luke (1702), **Leonard Ignatius** (1704-1789), Ann (1706). Will filed 9 October 1714, probated 12 January 1715 — bequeathed "Mount Misery" to son Thomas III, land to James, etc.

### Thomas Mattingly III — CONFIRMED (WikiTree Mattingly-28; will transcribed)

Thomas III (abt. 1688-1774) wrote his will 11 April 1774, probated 10 September 1774 in St. Mary's County. Explicitly names sons Leonard, Edward, John Baptist, and Clement as heirs. Also mentions "Mount Misery" plantation and "Mattingly's Lane" with a water mill. This will is the documentary anchor for the generation between Thomas II and the Kentucky migration era.

### Leonard Ignatius Mattingly (1704-1789) — PROBABLE

Leonard Ignatius, son of Thomas II, is referenced in FamilySearch's catalog of the Mattingly family genealogy book as "great-grandson of Thomas I who married twice and was the father of twelve children." He is likely one of the Mattingly progenitors who either migrated to Kentucky in 1785 with the Maryland Catholic cohort, or whose descendants did. The "Ignatius" given name reflects the strong Jesuit influence in Maryland's St. Mary's County Catholic community (St. Inigoes parish, named for St. Ignatius).

## Branch 3: Baity — "Pearl Baity" and the North Carolina Line

### Key finding: Pearl Baity's real name was Paralee

Documentary research resolves a central mystery: the "Pearl Baity" of family oral history is recorded in census and vital records as **Paralee (Johnson) Baity**. "Pearl" was almost certainly a family nickname for Paralee. Her husband was **William Alexander Baity** (WikiTree Baity-117).

### William Alexander Baity (1862-1945) — CONFIRMED (WikiTree Baity-117; 6 census records; Texas death certificate)

- Born 2 November 1862, Courtney, Yadkin County, North Carolina
- Grew up Liberty Township, Yadkin County (1870 and 1880 census confirmed)
- Married Paralee Johnson, 4 June 1899, Grayson County (Denison), Texas — matching the family oral history of coming to north Texas
- By 1900: in Denison, Grayson County (north Texas — Sherman/Denison area near Oklahoma border, near "Sherman's camp" oral history detail)
- By 1910: established in San Antonio — remained there through 1940 census
- Died 30 January 1945, San Antonio; Texas Death Certificate 477
- Children: **Ruth (Baity) Mattingly** and Margaret Baity

### The critical Baity-Mattingly link

Ruth (Baity) Mattingly is listed on WikiTree as William Baity's daughter. She **married a Mattingly** — the documentary bridge between the two family lines. Which specific Mattingly she married is not yet confirmed in accessible records, but the implication is that she married Leroy Teichmuller Mattingly or another Texas Mattingly of that generation. This is the open question most worth resolving next.

### Baity NC ancestry — CONFIRMED (WikiTree, 6 generations)

| Generation | Person | Dates | Location |
|-----------|--------|-------|----------|
| William Alexander Baity | The Texas builder | 1862-1945 | Yadkin NC → San Antonio TX |
| William D. Baity (father) | Farmer | 1829-1894 | North Carolina |
| Isham Baity (grandfather) | Farmer | 1804-1892 | Yadkin County NC |
| David Baity Sr. (g-grandfather) | Farmer | 1782-1856 | Surry County NC |
| George Baity Sr. (gg-grandfather) | Farmer | 1746-1828 | North Carolina |
| Rachel Allgood (gg-grandmother) | — | ~1756-? | North Carolina |

George Baity Sr. (born 1746) was alive during the American Revolution. His family line in Surry/Yadkin County NC aligns with the Appalachian Piedmont region — exactly the territory through which Sherman's Carolinas Campaign passed in 1865. The family's oral memory of a relative going by buckboard to beg Sherman not to burn their farm is consistent with this geography.

### Paralee Johnson's background

Her father Morgan Johnson was in Denison, Grayson County, Texas by 1900 (the 1900 census shows Paralee in his household before her marriage). Whether the Johnson family also came from NC is not confirmed. The migration to north Texas from the Carolinas in the 1870s is a well-documented pattern.

### Florence Butt / HEB corroboration — PROBABLE

**Florence Thornton Butt** (not "Herbert E. Butt" as Shari said — that was her grandson Howard E. Butt Jr.) founded the C.C. Butt Grocery Store in Kerrville, Texas on 26 November 1905. She was the mother of Howard Edward Butt Sr. (April 9, 1895 – March 12, 1991), who built H-E-B into a chain. The family was originally from Memphis, Tennessee — having moved to Kerrville due to Charles Butt's tuberculosis. Florence's Thornton family origins are not established in accessible sources; whether she grew up in the same NC Piedmont region as the Baity family would need further research.

## Branch 4: Frost Bank — Shari's Maternal Line

### The Frost Bank family succession — CONFIRMED (Wikipedia)

Frost Bank (founded 1868, San Antonio) has been Frost-family-controlled since founding:

1. **Thomas Claiborne Frost** (founder, 1868) — former Confederate Army lieutenant colonel
2. **T.C. Frost Jr.** (son) — ran the bank; constructed 12-story building 1921
3. **Joseph Hardin Frost** (brother of T.C. Jr.) — took over as president in 1926

If Shari's mother was born 1923 and her maternal uncle was "Frost Bank president," the most likely scenario is:
- Shari's grandmother was a sibling of T.C. Frost Jr. and Joseph Hardin Frost
- That would make Shari's grandmother a child of Thomas Claiborne Frost (founder)
- And Joseph Hardin Frost (president 1926) would be Shari's great-uncle, not uncle

The generational math: Thomas Claiborne Frost (founded bank 1868, so born ~1835-1845) → children born ~1860-1880 → grandchildren born ~1890-1910 → Shari's mother born 1923 would be Thomas Claiborne Frost's great-grandchild.

**The specific identity of Shari's Frost grandmother requires Shari to be asked directly.** Her mother's maiden name is the single most important piece of information needed to confirm this branch.

## Notes on the Centenarian (b. ~1828, d. ~1935)

The family oral history describes a man born around 1828 who lived to age 107 and died around 1935. The Baity line has William D. Baity (born 1829, died 1894 at age 65) — he is NOT the centenarian.

The centenarian is on the Mattingly paternal line. If Leroy Mattingly (b. 1898) is Shari's grandfather, his father Ed Mattingly was probably born ~1860-1870, Ed's father ~1835-1845, and Ed's grandfather ~1805-1820 — not quite reaching 1828. But if the family counts one more generation, an ancestor born 1828 who died 1935 at 107 is theoretically possible in the direct line.

This remains UNVERIFIED. A 107-year-old death in 1935 would have been national news in Texas newspapers. The Briscoe Center at UT Austin (which holds San Antonio Express-News microfilm) is the recommended next step.
