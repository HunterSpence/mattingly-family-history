## Executive Summary

The gap was **partially bridged at the near end.** This research confirms three Hampshire Mattingly persons in the Heckfield parish register (Phillimore's Transcript) beginning in 1548 — nearly 15 years earlier than the previously known John Mattingly (~1560). A second line of Mottingley/Mattingley persons appears in Wimbledon, Surrey from 1543. The 312-year core gap (1236–1543) remains without a named individual in any published primary source.

**Net result:** The near end of the gap has moved from ~1560 to ~1543. The far end remains firmly at ~1236.

## New Primary-Source Finds: Hampshire Mattingly 1538–1600

The Heckfield St Michael parish register (which covers the tithing of Mattingley) begins in 1538 per BHO VCH Hampshire vol. 4. Phillimore's Transcripts of this register are indexed in FreeREG and contain three confirmed Mattingly surname bearers:

### 1. Henry MATYNGLE — married Heckfield, 30 September 1548

The most significant find. Henry married at Heckfield St Michael, the mother church whose parochial jurisdiction covered Mattingley village itself. His name spelling "Matyngle" closely matches the 15th-century BHO place-name variant "Martyngle" — strongly suggesting his surname was derived locally from the village.

- **Source:** Phillimore's Transcript, Heckfield St Michael, Hampshire. Transcribed by Ken Greenslade, file line 48.
- **FreeREG:** https://www.freereg.org.uk/search_records/683737608655749cd6443497/
- **Estimated birth:** approximately 1518–1528
- **Bride name:** not captured in the transcript (blank)
- **Significance:** Born ~1518–1528, he is the right generation to be John Mattingly's (~1560) father. He is in exactly the right location. No primary source yet confirms the father-son relationship, but it is the most parsimonious hypothesis.

### 2. Agnes MATTYNGLY — married Heckfield, 10 September 1571

Agnes Mattyngly married Harry CAWTE at Heckfield St Michael.

- **Source:** Phillimore's Transcript, Heckfield St Michael. Transcribed by Ken Greenslade, file line 109.
- **FreeREG:** https://www.freereg.org.uk/search_records/683737658655749cd644359f/
- **Estimated birth:** approximately 1545–1551
- **Significance:** Chronologically consistent with being a daughter of Henry MATYNGLE (married 1548). She would have been baptised in the Heckfield registers ~1547–1553, but those baptism pages are not yet indexed in FreeREG.

### 3. Anne MATTINGLY — married Heckfield, 17 October 1596

Anne Mattingly married John AYER at Heckfield St Michael.

- **Source:** Phillimore's Transcript, Heckfield St Michael. Transcribed by Ken Greenslade, file line 230.
- **FreeREG:** https://www.freereg.org.uk/search_records/6837376b8655749cd64437a0/
- **Estimated birth:** approximately 1570–1576
- **Significance:** Third-generation Heckfield Mattingly in the same register. Consistent with being a daughter of Agnes MATTYNGLY (1571) or another child of Henry MATYNGLE.

## New Primary-Source Finds: Surrey Mottingley 1543–1566

### 4. Thomas MOTTINGLEY — baptised Wimbledon St Mary, 16 January 1542/43

**Father:** Henry MOTTINGLEY. **Mother:** Joan.

- **Source:** Other Transcript (FreeREG), transcribed by Sandra Gould Seenan, file line 40.
- **FreeREG:** https://www.freereg.org.uk/search_records/6858301c865574d283029a69/
- **Significance:** This is the earliest individual bearing a Mattingley-type surname found in any parish register — 1543, Wimbledon, Surrey. His father Henry MOTTINGLEY (born approximately 1510–1525) is a named individual approximately one generation earlier than Thomas. Wimbledon is 25 miles from Heckfield.

### 5. Elizabeth MOTTINGLEY — baptised Wimbledon St Mary, 20 January 1565/66

**Father:** Thomas MOTTINGLEY (the same Thomas baptised in 1543).

- **Source:** Other Transcript (FreeREG), transcribed by Sandra Gould Seenan, file line 226.
- **FreeREG:** https://www.freereg.org.uk/search_records/68583023865574d283029d44/
- **Significance:** Confirms Thomas MOTTINGLEY survived to adulthood (~age 22) and had children in Wimbledon. He is NOT confirmed as connected to John Mattingly (~1560) or the Heckfield Hampshire community.

**Caveat:** The Wimbledon records are "Other Transcript" source type — not Phillimore's and not the original register. The underlying source should be verified at Surrey History Centre (Woking). The FreeREG transcriber is Sandra Gould Seenan but the original document type is unspecified.

## London/Middlesex Mattingley Persons (16th–17th Century)

A cluster of Mattingley/Mathingley persons appears at Westminster St Margaret in the 1580s–1590s, most associated with a man named Roger MATTINGLEY:

- Fraunces MATHINGLEY bap. 27 Mar 1580 — father Roger MATHINGLEY
- Robert MATTINGLEY bap. 3 Sep 1581 — father Roger MATTINGLEY
- Richard MATTINGLEY bap. 5 Dec 1582 — father Roger MATTINGLEY
- Ester MOTTINGLEY bap. 8 Apr 1588 — father Roger MOTTINGLEY

A second family appears at the same church:
- Anne MATTINGLEIGH bap. 8 Dec 1582 — father Robert MATTINGLEIGH
- Jane MATTINGLEY bap. 11 May 1589 — father Wylliam MATTINGLEY

Roger MATTINGLEY (born estimated 1545–1560) is a contemporary of John Mattingly (~1560). They could be brothers, cousins, or unrelated. No document connects Roger to the Heckfield Hampshire community or to John's line. Westminster St Margaret is approximately 2 miles from St Mary Colechurch where John Mattingly was buried in 1641.

Also confirmed: Robert MATTINGLEY married Susan PAYNTER at Clerkenwell, Middlesex, 23 May 1574.

All Westminster and Clerkenwell records are "Other Transcript" source type in FreeREG (transcriber: Judy Brett or Jean Oakes).

## The 300-Year Core Gap: Still Unbridged

### What the BHO VCH Hampshire Actually Says

The Victoria County History of Hampshire, vol. 4, pp. 44–51 (published 1911, based on direct archival research) is unambiguous:

> "MATTINGLEY (Matingeleghe, xi cent.; Matingele, xiii cent.; Martyngle, xv cent.) ... Ellis, the owner in 1167... apparently succeeded by Revelendus, lord of Mattingley... Stephen de Mattingley, who in 1206 granted to Walter Prior of Merton... Stephen was succeeded by his son Peter de Mattingley, who sold all his property to Geoffrey de Arundel."

After Geoffrey de Arundel's acknowledgment in 1236 (Feet of Fines Hants Mich. 21 Hen. III), the Rycher family appears at Mattingley by the mid-14th century (Feet of Fines Hants Hil. 14 Edw. III, ~1340) — a DIFFERENT surname. No de Mattingley or Mattingly person appears in the VCH narrative between ~1236 and the 1538 opening of the parish registers.

**The gap from ~1236 to 1543 = approximately 307 years.** No named Mattingly individual has been found in any published primary source for this period.

### Why This Gap May Be Permanent in Open Sources

After Peter de Mattingley sold the manor (~1215–1235), the surname-bearing family ceased to be lords. They likely continued as tenant farmers in the village — generating no land transactions, no Feet of Fines, no manorial court appearances that would leave records distinguishable from the mass of unnamed tenants. The medieval taxation records (E 179 lay subsidies) would contain them by name but are not indexed by name in online databases; individual-level data requires in-person research at Kew.

## Pre-1167 Context: Domesday to Ellis

| Person | Date | Role | Source |
|--------|------|------|--------|
| Alric | fl. 1066 | Held Mattingley of Edward the Confessor as alod (free tenure) | Domesday Book 1086 / VCH Hants i, 505a |
| Alsi son of Brixi (Alfsi son of Brictsi) | 1086 | Held Mattingley under Hugh de Port (post-Conquest) | Domesday Book 1086 / VCH Hants i, 505a |
| [unnamed] | 1086–1167 | 81-year gap with no named Mattingley lord identified | — |
| Ellis | fl. 1167 | Owner of Mattingley | Pipe Roll 13 Henry II (Pipe Roll Soc. xi, 189) |

The village name etymology: "Matta's leah" — Old English for "clearing of the people/followers of Matta." The -inga- element indicates a family or group. Alric is a village-level figure documented only in Domesday as a property holder; he is not a proven genealogical ancestor of any later Mattingly family. His presence establishes the pre-Conquest Anglo-Saxon farming community.

Between Domesday (1086) and Ellis (fl. 1167) there is a gap of approximately 81 years with no named lord in published sources. The Norman Conquest restructured landholding; Mattingley passed into the hands of Hugh de Port (a major Hampshire Norman baron), and Alsi son of Brixi held it under him.

## What Was NOT Found

**FreeREG Hampshire baptisms:** Zero Mattingly baptisms in the FreeREG index for Hampshire 1538–1640. This likely reflects incomplete indexing of the Heckfield registers (the marriage pages of the Phillimore transcript are indexed; baptism pages apparently are not), not actual absence of records. The physical Hampshire Record Office registers are the next step.

**TNA E 179 lay subsidy rolls:** The E179 database does not index individual taxpayer names. The Hampshire Holdshott hundred rolls for 1290–1534 exist in TNA at Kew but cannot be searched by surname online. No published transcription of this specific series was found in open-access sources.

**PCC wills (PROB 11):** No Mattingly wills found in open catalog search for 1383–1560. Catalog metadata does not index individual testator names; requires DocumentsOnline search or direct archive access.

**BHO Calendar of Patent Rolls / Close Rolls (Henry III through Richard II):** Paywalled. Cannot confirm or deny Mattingly appearances.

## Most Viable Hypothesis (Not Proven)

Henry MATYNGLE (married Heckfield 1548, born approximately 1518–1528) is the strongest candidate for John Mattingly's (~1560) father, based on:
1. Geography: Heckfield/Mattingley is John's reputed birthplace region
2. Chronology: Henry born ~1518–1528 could have a son born ~1560 at age 32–42
3. Location: His marriage was in the church whose register covers the Mattingley tithing
4. Name: His spelling "Matyngle" maps directly to the BHO 15th-century place-name variant

This hypothesis requires verification against the original Heckfield baptism register (1538–1575 book, held at Hampshire Record Office) to find a baptism of "John Mati[n]gly" or similar circa 1555–1565.

## Honest Assessment

**What was achieved:** The near end of the gap was pushed back 12–17 years — from John Mattingly (~1560) to Henry MATYNGLE (fl. 1548) and Thomas MOTTINGLEY (bap. 1543). A plausible hypothesis now exists for John's father (Henry MATYNGLE, Heckfield 1548), but it is not yet proven by primary source.

**What remains unknown:** The 307-year void from Peter de Mattingley (~1236) to Henry Matyngle (~1548) contains zero named individuals in any published primary source. Whether this represents genuine documentary absence or simply unindexed records is unknowable without in-person archive work.

**What the honest tree looks like:** Stop at John Mattingly (~1560, buried 1641), note Henry MATYNGLE (Heckfield 1548) as a strongly probable but unconfirmed parent, and treat everything before 1548 as conjecture not supported by open-access primary sources.
