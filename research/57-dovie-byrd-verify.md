## Geographic Consistency Analysis

The hypothesis is geographically consistent with the era:

- John A Byrd (1868-1928) is confirmed in Milam County, TX (Sharp is a small community in Milam County near the Brazos River).
- Otha Lee Byrd Sr. (1902-1989) ended up in Clute, Brazoria County — roughly 130 miles southeast of Milam County, near Freeport on the Gulf Coast. This is consistent with the 1920s-1940s economic migration pattern from Central Texas toward the oil and chemical industry corridor along the Gulf Coast.
- If Dovie married a Spence and moved to Beaumont (Jefferson County), that is also consistent — Beaumont is approximately 100 miles east of Clute along the Gulf Coast. The oil boom (Spindletop 1901) drew workers from across Central Texas to both the Beaumont and Brazoria corridors.
- The movement pattern Milam Co → Beaumont/Clute area is documented for many Central Texas families in this era.

**Confidence for geographic consistency: PROBABLE**
