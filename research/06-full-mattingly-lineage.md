# Mattingly Direct Paternal Lineage: Reconstruction
**From Thomas Mattingly (pre-1665 Maryland) to Hunter Spence**
*Research conducted April 2026*

---

## Summary

This document traces the direct paternal Mattingly line across 15 generations. Approximately 9 of those generations are confirmed or probable from primary sources. The critical unresolved gap is the Kentucky-to-Texas migration (roughly 1800–1870), covering an estimated 3–4 generations for which no documentary sources have yet been found.

**Key finding:** The earliest confirmed American Mattingly is **Thomas Mattingly II** (abt.1650–bef.1715), who arrived in St. Mary's County, Maryland prior to 1665 with his wife Elizabeth and children. The family was Catholic and appears on the Maryland Rent Rolls continuously from 1663. The surname traces to Mattingley village, Hampshire, England — "Matta's ley" in Old English.

## The Confirmed Lineage (Well-Documented Generations)

### England: Pre-1650

The surname is Anglo-Saxon, from Mattingley, Hampshire (Hart District, England — 51°19'N, 0°57'W). The earliest ancestors with documented names are:

- **Unnamed ancestor (~1560)** — appears in WikiTree 2nd-great-grandparent rows
- **Francis Mattingly** — next generation, no dates known
- **Charles Mattingly** (~1600, Omny Parish, Sussex, England) — appears as "abt 1600 - abt 1642" in WikiTree profiles for multiple Maryland Mattinglys; named in the 1969 genealogy book as a possible "1633 ship carpenter immigrant" though the exact relationship is uncertain
- **Thomas Mattingly I** (~1624 – ~24 Jul 1664, Omny, Sussex, England) — died in England before his son emigrated; his wife was Elizabeth Unknown (born ~1626)

The 1969 book *The Descendants of Henry Mattingly* notes: "The earliest definite traces locate the family near Dublin, Ireland, whence some of them migrated to America." This Irish connection — possibly via the Catholic diaspora — may explain the "definite Irish traditions" the book references alongside the English surname origin.

### The Maryland Immigrant: Thomas Mattingly II (~1650–1715)

**WikiTree profiles confirming this person:** Mattingly-60, Mattingly-66, Mattingly-210, Mattingly-250, Mattingly-53

Thomas Mattingly II (abt.1650 – bef.12 Jan 1715) emigrated to Maryland prior to 1665. The *Descendants of Henry Mattingly* (1969) states: "Thomas Mattingly, his wife, Elizabeth, and children, Thomas, Cezar and Elizabeth, came to Maryland prior to 1665." He and a William Mattingly appear on the St. Mary's County Rent Rolls starting in 1663 — records preserved at the Hall of Records, Annapolis, Maryland.

Thomas II settled on "St. Thomas Place" in St. Mary's County and his name appears on those Rent Rolls from 1663 to 1720. He married Elizabeth (Ely) Mattingly (~1654–1714 in St. Mary's County). His children included Ignatius (~1704), James (~1696), and others.

**Note on oral tradition vs. records:** Shari's interview claims Thomas Mattingly "arrived with Lord Baltimore in 1660 and died 1662." The Rent Rolls show a Thomas Mattingly alive 1663–1720. The most likely explanation: Shari's oral tradition slightly compressed/conflated the immigrant story — the actual arrival was pre-1665, and Thomas II lived until ~1715. Lord Baltimore's Catholic colony (chartered 1632, settled 1634) is the correct historical context, and the family was definitely part of that Catholic Maryland community.

No "Mattingly's Hope" land patent has been confirmed in the sources consulted — this name may refer to a different Mattingly family line or be a family oral tradition that cannot be verified with available sources.

### Generation 3: Ignatius Mattingly (abt.1704–1789)

**WikiTree:** Mattingly-60

Son of Thomas II. Born ~1704 in St. Mary's County, died April 1789 in St. Mary's County. Married Sarah Catherine Fowler (~1705–1795). His will (probated 1789, St. Mary's County Will Book JJ1) named sons Leonard, Luke, William, and daughters Elizabeth, Sarah, Susanna, Hannah. Sons Leonard and Luke were the progenitors of the Catholic Kentucky Mattingly families.

### Generation 4: Leonard Mattingly Sr. (1739–1829)

Son of Ignatius. Born 1739 in Leonardtown, St. Mary's County, Maryland; died abt. 28 September 1829 in Leonardtown. Married Mary (Hayden) Mattingly (1739–1812). His children included Leonard Jr. (1764–1843), John Baptist (~1762–1821), and Ignatius (~1766–1851) — all of whom migrated to Kentucky.

### Generation 5: Leonard Mattingly Jr. (1764–1843)

**WikiTree:** Mattingly-210 | **FamilySearch marriage record:** V5ZD-6SJ

Son of Leonard Sr. Born 1764 in Leonardtown, St. Mary's County, Maryland. Migrated to Kentucky by 1788 — married Ann (Cissell) Mattingly in Nelson County, Kentucky on 7 January 1788. Settled in Marion County, Kentucky (the community of Saint Mary). Died 1843, buried in Saint Mary, Marion County, Kentucky.

Leonard Jr. is the **genetic common ancestor** confirmed by the Mattingly-Matney Y-chromosome DNA Surname Project. Multiple DNA participants trace directly to him.

The 1969 book notes: "The Kentucky Mattinglys trace their ancestry to Charles, whose sons, William, Leonard and Luke, settled in Hardin Creek, Kentucky, in 1786." Marion County, Kentucky — near Bardstown — became the heart of Catholic Kentucky, sometimes called the "Holy Land of Kentucky."

Leonard Jr.'s children by Ann Cissell included: Mary (Mattingly) Alvey, Henry Martin Mattingly Sr., William Cissell Mattingly, Leonard Mattingly III.

## The Critical Gap: Kentucky to Texas (Generations 6–9, ~1800–1890)

This is the most poorly documented portion of the lineage. No documentary sources have yet been found for the individual(s) who migrated from Marion County, Kentucky to San Antonio, Texas. Historical context suggests this migration would have occurred between approximately 1820 (Texas land grant era) and 1860 (pre-Civil War).

**Oral tradition anchor:** Shari described a great-great-great-grandfather who "lived to age 107, born 1828, died 1935." This is extraordinary enough that newspaper coverage should exist. If confirmed, this person (born 1828) would be a grandchild of Leonard Jr. (1764–1843) — fitting neatly as a second-generation Kentucky Mattingly who later moved to Texas. His 1935 death in Texas would generate an obituary that could name parents and children.

**Recommended next research steps for this gap:**
1. Texas General Land Office records (1820–1860): search for Mattingly headright land grants
2. San Fernando Cathedral records (San Antonio): Mattingly baptisms/marriages ~1840–1880
3. 1850/1860/1880 Texas Census: Bexar County (San Antonio) for Mattingly households
4. San Antonio Express-News archives, 1935: obituary search for a 107-year-old Mattingly
5. Texas death certificates, 1935: surname Mattingly, age 107, county Bexar or adjacent

## The Confirmed Texas Generations

### Generation 10: The Texas Judge (Unknown Name)

Shari described her great-grandfather as "a judge in Texas" who was the father of Leroy Teichmuller Mattingly (b.1898). He married a German-American woman from the Teichmuller family — confirmed by Leroy's distinctive middle name and the presence of Minette Teichmueller (1871–1970), the WPA muralist, as Shari's "Aunt Monette, my grandfather's mother" (likely Leroy's mother's sister or aunt).

The Teichmuller family were German immigrants settled in La Grange, Fayette County, Texas (Minette was born there in 1871) and later San Antonio. Marriage records for a Mattingly–Teichmuller union in Bexar County or Fayette County, Texas (~1890–1900) have not yet been searched.

Per the Shari interview, he "built half of San Antonio" (likely her description of his construction/builder business, not judicial work) and died in 1945. He may be the same person as p010 in entities.json ("great-grandfather, a very successful builder who built half of San Antonio; died in 1945").

**Note:** Shari may have two different great-grandparents merged here — the judge (Leroy's father) and the builder (the man who died 1945). They may be the same person or different people (e.g., the judge was Leroy's grandfather and the builder was his father).

### Generation 11: Leroy Teichmuller Mattingly (born 1898)

**Most reliably documented Texas-generation anchor.**

Born 1898, likely in San Antonio. University of Texas engineering graduate (~1920s). His middle name "Teichmuller" directly encodes his mother's maiden name. His siblings:
- **Claude Mattingly** (~born ~1900): physician in San Antonio; found dead in a hotel room with a University of Texas professor's wife in 1934 — significant local scandal that should be findable in San Antonio newspaper archives
- **Mamie Mattingly** (~born 1900–1902): their mother died in childbirth at her delivery

Leroy married Shari's grandmother (born ~1900–1902, attended North Texas State University, lost family wealth in the 1929 crash).

### Generation 12: Shari's Father (born 1922, San Antonio)

Name unknown from available records. Born 1922 in San Antonio. Had a master's degree. Had siblings including "Billy Mattingly" (geologist, Stanford graduate). Son of Leroy Teichmuller Mattingly.

### Generation 13: Sharyn ("Shari") Mattingly (born ~1947)

Currently ~79 years old, Santa Monica, California. Royal owner of eight oil wells in Reeves County, Texas. Married David (Hunter's paternal grandfather). Children include Charmaine and Hunter's parent.

### Generations 14–15: Hunter's Parent and Hunter Spence (living)
