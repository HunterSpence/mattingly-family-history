## STATUS UPGRADE: Hans–Gustav Sibling Relationship

**Previous confidence: PROBABLE (0.82)**

**New confidence: CONFIRMED**

The 2016 Neue Deutsche Biographie (NDB) article for Gustav Teichmüller explicitly lists in its genealogy section:

> "2 B Wilhelm (1834–69, ⚭ Bertha Kuntzen), Premierlt., Schriftst., Hans (1837–1901, ⚭ Auguste Kellner), Jur., Offz., Friedensrichter in Texas"

Translation: "2 brothers: Wilhelm (1834–69, m. Bertha Kuntzen), Premier-Lieutenant, writer; Hans (1837–1901, m. Auguste Kellner), jurist, officer, justice of the peace in Texas"

This is a peer-reviewed scholarly article in Germany's national biography series (published by the Historical Commission of the Bavarian Academy of Sciences), written by Heiner Schwenke in 2016. It constitutes a definitive archival confirmation that Hans Teichmüller (1837–1901, Hunter's great-great-grandfather) and Gustav Teichmüller (1832–1888, philosopher) were brothers.

## Question-by-Question Findings

### Q1: August Teichmüller's Parish Baptism

**Status: PROBABLE (NDB biographical data, no original parish record retrieved)**

- **Full name:** August Wilhelm Teichmüller
- **Born:** 1795, Braunschweig (Duchy of Brunswick)
- **Died:** 1855, cause unclear (death brought financial hardship to family)
- **Occupation:** Seconde-Lieutenant ("Zweiter Leutnant") in the **Schwarzen Corps des Majors Olfermann** — a Brunswick infantry unit in the post-Napoleonic reorganization of the Duchy's army. The NDB uses this specific designation, which corresponds to one of the Brunswick line infantry battalions of the 1820s–1840s.
- **Father:** Wilhelm Ernst Conrad Teichmüller (1758–1835) — see Q6
- **Mother:** Henriette Christiane Helene Schorkopf (1763–1818), from Uslar (Niedersachsen)
- **GND (German National Library identifier):** 1089914733
- **Source:** NDB 26 (2016), p. 6* — genealogy section of Gustav Teichmüller article

Note on prior research: Previous research cited the Lotto 1902 primary source as "officer in the Brunswick army" and Wikipedia as "lieutenant in the Prussian army." The NDB gives the most precise information: Seconde-Lieutenant in the Schwarzen Corps (a Brunswick unit, not Prussian). Lotto 1902's "Brunswick army" is correct.

Original baptism record: Almost certainly held by **Niedersächsisches Landesarchiv (NLA), Wolfenbüttel branch** in the church registers of one of Braunschweig's Lutheran parishes (St. Martin, St. Andreas, St. Aegidien, or the Dom). Not yet digitized in any accessible online repository as of April 2026.

### Q7: Teichmüller Military Dynasty in Brunswick

**Status: CONFIRMED (limited to August Wilhelm; broader dynasty PROBABLE)**

**Confirmed military Teichmüllers (Brunswick):**
- August Wilhelm (1795–1855): Seconde-Lieutenant, Schwarzen Corps des Majors Olfermann
- Wilhelm (1834–1869): Premier-Lieutenant, writer (Gustav's brother, Hunter's uncle)

**The Schwarzen Corps context:** This is a post-Napoleonic Brunswick infantry formation. The "Black Brunswickers" (Schwarze Schar) who fought at Waterloo (1815) were reorganized into regular Brunswick infantry battalions in the 1810s–1820s. A "Schwarzen Corps des Majors Olfermann" is a specific unit designation. Major [surname] Olfermann cannot be further identified from accessible online sources, but this level of specificity is typical of NDB genealogy entries that draw on Braunschweig military personnel files (held at NLA Wolfenbüttel under Bestand 26 or similar).

**Assessment:** Two brothers (August and his son Wilhelm) both served as military officers in Brunswick. The pattern suggests the family had officer-class connections but was not a multi-generation military dynasty in the same way as the von Girsewald family. The Teichmüller line was more a mining/administrative family that obtained military commissions in one generation before branching into law (Hans in Texas) and philosophy (Gustav).

### Q9: Robert Teichmüller (pianist, 1863–1939)

**Status: POSSIBLE connection to Hans/Gustav family — NOT confirmed**

- **Born:** 4 May 1863, Braunschweig
- **Died:** 6 May 1939, Leipzig
- **Occupation:** Concert pianist, Leipzig Conservatory professor (1897 onward, professor from 1908)
- **GND:** 11927213X

**Analysis of family connection:**

The NDB genealogy for Gustav lists brother Wilhelm Teichmüller (1834–1869) as having married Bertha Kuntzen (also listed in GND as "Teichmüller-Specht, Bertha von"). Robert Teichmüller (born 1863 in Braunschweig) fits the timeline to be Wilhelm's son — Wilhelm died 1869 when Robert would have been 6 years old. This is the most parsimonious hypothesis.

The 2016 NDB article for Gustav does NOT list Robert among Gustav's children (they are named separately). The NDB genealogy for Joachim the electrotechnician (1866–1938) does NOT mention Robert either.

**Verdict:** Robert Teichmüller was probably a son of Wilhelm Teichmüller (1834–69) and thus Gustav's nephew and Hans's nephew — but this is POSSIBLE, not CONFIRMED. The definitive source would be the **Braunschweigisches Biographisches Lexikon, 19. und 20. Jahrhundert** (1996), p. 606, cited in Robert's German Wikipedia article but not digitized online. Also NLA Wolfenbüttel baptism records for Braunschweig, 1863.

## Notable Teichmüller Members — Biographical Summary

### Gustav Teichmüller (philosopher, 1832–1888) — CONFIRMED as Hans's brother

- Born 19 November 1832 in Braunschweig; died 22 May 1888 in Dorpat (now Tartu, Estonia)
- German philosopher; influenced Friedrich Nietzsche's perspectivism
- Professor at University of Basel (1868–1871), then Imperial University of Dorpat (1871–1888)
- Married twice: (1) Anna Cramer (1842–1862), daughter of Estonian landowner Georg Wilhelm Cramer (1812–82); (2) Lina Cramer (1844–1894), his sister-in-law
- 10 children total (2 from first marriage, 8 from second)
- Died of stomach cancer aged 55
- A street in Braunschweig was named after him in 1975
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustav_Teichm%C3%BCller

### Anna Teichmüller (composer, 1861–1940) — CONFIRMED as Gustav's daughter

- Born 11 May 1861 in Göttingen; died 6 September 1940
- Gustav's eldest daughter by first wife Anna Cramer
- Composer and pianist; lived at the Schreiberhau artist colony (now Szklarska Poręba, Poland)
- Associated with Carl Hauptmann; composed over 43 opuses including songs, chamber music, and one opera
- Wikipedia: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anna_Teichm%C3%BCller

### Robert Teichmüller (pianist, 1863–1939) — POSSIBLE nephew of Hans (unconfirmed)

- Born 4 May 1863 in Braunschweig; died 6 May 1939 in Leipzig
- Concert pianist, Leipzig Conservatory professor 1897–1939
- Students included Günther Ramin, Sigfrid Karg-Elert, Eileen Joyce
- Source for family connection: Braunschweigisches Biographisches Lexikon (1996), p. 606 — not digitized
- If son of Wilhelm (1834–69), would be Hunter's great-great-uncle's son = 1st cousin 3× removed

### Joachim Teichmüller (electrotechnician, 1866–1938) — 4th cousin branch

- Born 4 March 1866 in Bernburg (Saale); died 17 June 1938 in Marburg
- Pioneer of lighting technology ("Lichtarchitektur"); founded first Lichttechnisches Institut in Germany (Karlsruhe, 1922)
- Cousin branch only (shares ancestor Joachim Andreas 1705–78 but diverged before 1730)
- NDB 26 (2016), p. 7*; https://www.deutsche-biographie.de/gnd101258223X.html

### For original Teichmüller parish records (baptisms, marriages, deaths in Braunschweig):

- Holdings: Braunschweig church registers (Lutheran) from the major city parishes

## What Remains Unverified

1. **Original baptism records** for Hans (1837), Gustav (1832), and August Wilhelm (1795) — NDB gives dates and parents, but parish register images are not digitized online. Held at NLA Wolfenbüttel. Verification requires in-person visit or paid research service.

2. **The unnamed generation between Joachim Andreas (1705–78) and Wilhelm Ernst Conrad (1758–1835)** — NDB identifies Joachim Andreas as the great-great-grandfather of Gustav (philosopher), implying one unnamed intervening generation.

3. **Joachim Andreas's own parents** — Not named in any accessible source. May be traceable through Goslar church registers (Goslar-St.Stephan, Goslar-Marktkirche) at NLA Hannover or the Stadtarchiv Goslar.

4. **Charlotte von Girsewald's exact position in the Girsewald family** — NDB gives her father as Georg (1735–1816) + Christine Elisabeth Boyer, but does not name her in relation to the well-documented Freiherrliche von Girsewald line (Conrad Alexander Gustav, 1785). The Gothaisches Genealogisches Taschenbuch (1921) resolves this. PROBABLE that she was a sibling of the 1785 Freiherr.

5. **Robert Teichmüller's parentage** — POSSIBLE son of Wilhelm (1834–69). Requires BBL 1996 p. 606 or NLA Wolfenbüttel baptism register for Braunschweig, ca. 1863.

6. **The unnamed Teichmüller sister who died early** — NDB says "1 früh †" (1 died young). No name, dates, or further information found.

7. **Wilhelm Teichmüller's wife discrepancy** — NDB text says "Bertha Kuntzen" but GND lists "Teichmüller-Specht, Bertha von." These may be the same woman with a variant maiden name recorded differently in two sources, or a transcription error.

8. **Wilhelmina (Minette) Teichmüller (1829–86, Gustav's sister)** — Newly identified in this research. No prior source had found her. She married Karl Mollenhauer (1818–1864), Protestant pastor and Superintendent in Bockenem/Ambergau, Niedersachsen. No Wikidata entry found. She would be Hunter's great-great-great-aunt.
