## HEADLINE FIND: Edward Mattingly Sr.'s Parents — The KY→TX Bridge CLOSED

**This is the single highest-value result of this research session.**

WikiTree Mattingly-596 directly names Edward Mattingly Sr.'s parents:

- **Father:** Thomas Jefferson Mattingly (WikiTree Mattingly-591) — born 1828, Kentucky; died 1883, Pattonsburg, Daviess, Missouri
- **Mother:** Elizabeth Christie (WikiTree Christie-1829) — born 2 Dec 1833, Christianburg, Shelby, Kentucky

Thomas Jefferson's father was **Ignatius Mattingly** (Mattingly-140, born abt. 1781 St. Mary's MD, died 1833 Richmond, Madison, KY), who was a son of **Ignatius Mattingly Jr.** (Mattingly-141), who was a son of **Leonard Mattingly Sr.** (Mattingly-62, 1739–abt.1829), who was a son of **Ignatius Mattingly** (Mattingly-60, abt. 1704–1789).

The complete resolved chain:

> Ignatius Mattingly (1704) → Leonard Sr. (1739) → Ignatius Jr. (1750) → Ignatius (1781, KY) → Thomas Jefferson (1828, KY → MO) → **Edward Mattingly Sr.** (1868, MO → TX) → Leroy (1896, La Grange TX)

The family left Kentucky for Missouri after Ignatius (Mattingly-140) died in 1833. Thomas Jefferson was raised in Missouri, where he married twice. His son Edward was born 6 September 1868 in Missouri. Edward moved to Texas by 1894 (marriage to May Teichmueller in La Grange).

**Edward also married a second time:** Blanche D. Schroeder (born 1 Jul 1886, Missouri), in Fayette County, Texas. This marriage produced a fourth child: **Edward Mattingly Jr.** (b. abt. 1915, La Grange) — a half-sibling of Claude, Leroy, and Mamie.

Source confidence: CONFIRMED — WikiTree Mattingly-596, Mattingly-591, Mattingly-140.

## Open Questions

1. **Teichmueller 5th child** — 1880 US Census, Fayette County TX, Hans Teichmueller household on FamilySearch is the targeted source. If the child died between 1862 and 1870, they may also appear in that window.

2. **Thomas Mattingly III wife-child assignment** — Will 1774 names Leonard, Edward, John Baptist, Clement. WikiTree shows both a Clement Mattingly (Mattingly-384, by Warren) and a Clement (Mattingly) Matney (Mattingly-611, by Cole). Maryland State Archives will transcript needed to clarify.

3. **Leonard Mattingly Sr. wives/children split** — 3 wives, 14 children. Which children belong to which wife not fully resolved. Dorothy Hardesty entry has reduced editorial confidence on WikiTree.

4. **David Baity Sr. spouse** — Completely unknown. NC State Archives (Surry/Yadkin County deeds, estates 1800–1860) needed.

5. **George Baity Sr. child-mother attribution** — Formally confirmed only for George Jr. (Rachel Allgood). Other three children's maternity is undocumented.

6. **Thomas Jefferson Mattingly's mother** — WikiTree Mattingly-591 shows [mother unknown]. 1850 Madison County KY census and Kentucky State Archives marriage records needed.

7. **George Thomas Mattingly (Mattingly-695, b.1830)** — WikiTree shows [spouse(s) unknown], [children unknown], no death record. He is no longer the primary candidate for the KY-TX bridge (that is now confirmed as Thomas Jefferson Mattingly → Edward Sr.). But his fate remains documented nowhere.

8. **Centenarian born 1828** — Thomas Jefferson Mattingly born 1828 KY died 1883 MO at age ~54. He does not match. No other Mattingly born 1828 found. The oral tradition figure remains unidentified.
