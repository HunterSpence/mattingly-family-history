## 3. The English Spence Surname — Geographic Distribution

### Where Spences Are Concentrated in England (CONFIRMED — Forebears.io, HouseOfNames)

**Surname origin:** Yorkshire, England — Simon del Spens first documented at **Gisborough Priory**, North Yorkshire (now Redcar and Cleveland), in **1300** (Charters of Guisburn Priory). King Edward I's reign. This is NE England, in the Cleveland Hills / Teesside region.

**1881 UK Census distribution (PROBABLE — SurnameDB, Forebears.io):**
- England total: ~5,671 Spences (rank 646th in England)
- Scotland total: ~4,212 Spences (rank 149th in Scotland)

The name is significantly more concentrated per capita in Scotland (rank 149) than England (rank 646). Within England, the historical and geographical evidence points to:

1. **Yorkshire (North Riding / West Riding / East Riding):** The surname's first English documentary record is Yorkshire (1300). Yorkshire convicts transported to Australia include John Spence (convicted York, Yorkshire, transported 1800) and John Spence (West Riding, Yorkshire, transported 1837). The name clusters in NE England where Scottish borders meet.

2. **County Durham / Northumberland (NE England):** Thomas Spence (1750-1814), English radical reformer, was born in **Newcastle upon Tyne** to a **Presbyterian family** — indicating Scottish/NE English descent. His father Andrew Spence was likely a Scottish-descended craftsman. Mr. Tristram F Spence (born 1910), from **Low Fell, County Durham**, died in the HMS Hood sinking (1941) — a documented 20th-century Spence from County Durham. "Low Fell" is in Gateshead, County Durham, adjacent to Newcastle.

3. **Fife, Scotland:** The Clan Spens heartland. The name is 190th most common in Scotland vs. 646th in England, showing heavier Scottish concentration. NE England Spences often had Scottish ancestry via the border migrations.

### The Name's Dual Identity (CONFIRMED — multiple sources)

The Spence surname has a dual heritage: documented earliest in Yorkshire (1300, English record) but the principal family line in Scotland (Clan Spens of Fife, chartered from 1161). Both streams are the same occupational root (Old French *despense* — the pantry/larder keeper). The distinction:

- **English Spences:** Descended from Yorkshire/NE England stock; the surname spread through Northern England near the Scottish border; Presbyterian-tinged family culture typical of the region
- **Scottish Spens/Spence:** The Lowland clan of Fife; more concentrated; hereditary in specific estates (Lathallan, Craigsanquhar, Wormieston)

For an English-born Dale William Spence Sr., the most likely regional origin is **NE England (Yorkshire, County Durham, or Northumberland)** given surname distribution patterns — or possibly Scotland itself (with the family having an English address in the 20th century).

### 4C. The Norman French Origin (CONFIRMED — multiple surname dictionaries)

Both the English and Scottish Spence/Spens lines trace to the same Norman French root:

**Etymology chain:**
- Latin: *dispendere* = "to weigh out / to distribute"
- Medieval Latin: *dispensa* = "the larder/pantry"
- Old French: *despense* (Anglo-French: *espense*) = "the storeroom, buttery"
- Middle English: *spence*, *spens* = "the larder"
- Occupational role: The *spenser* or *despenser* = "the household steward controlling the food storeroom"
- Surname: Initially *de la Despense* or *atte Spence* → reduced to Spens/Spence

The most famous medieval *Despenser* family is the Le Despenser dynasty (Norman-English nobility), including Hugh le Despenser, 1st Earl of Winchester (1261-1326) and his son Hugh le Despenser the Younger (1286-1326), favourites of King Edward II — a direct parallel to the occupational lineage of the surname. The Clan Spens and common Spence families are the commoner branch of the same occupational designation.

**Source:** *A Dictionary of English and Welsh Surnames* (Bardsley, 1896); *The Surnames of Scotland* (Black, 1946); *Patronymica Britannica* (Lower, 1860); *Surnames of the United Kingdom* (Harrison, 1912) — all via HouseOfNames.com and SurnameDB.
