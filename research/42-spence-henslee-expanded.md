## Early America arrivals and older surname context

### Spence surname in early Virginia

- Lyman Carrier's *Agriculture in Virginia, 1607-1699* states that William Spence arrived on the *Phoenix* on 20 April 1608, describes him as the first experienced English farmer in Virginia, and notes that he served in the first House of Burgesses in 1619.
- A separate 1624 Virginia census transcription lists Sarah Spence at James Island.
- Confidence: HIGH for the records themselves; LOW for any direct connection to Hunter's branch
- Sources:
  - http://www.gutenberg.org/cache/epub/28730/pg28730-images.html
  - https://www.cynthiaswope.com/withinthevines/jamestown/jamestowncensus1624.html
  - https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Spence_%28burgess%29

### Henslee surname context

- Ancestry's surname page treats Henslee as an English variant of Hensley and notes that the surname was already present in the United States by 1840, with Alabama the largest early concentration in that census year.
- Confidence: LOW to MEDIUM as surname context; not a proven direct line for Hunter
- Source:
  - https://www.ancestry.com/last-name-meaning/henslee
