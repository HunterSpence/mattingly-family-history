## Summary of Findings

The Lepick surname in this family is a Bohemian/Czech spelling variant of the Czech surname **Lepík**, introduced into the United States by Frank Lepik (Lepick) who immigrated from Bohemia (then Austria-Hungary) in 1881 and settled in Brown County, Kansas. His son Frederick Charles Lepick Sr. was born in Kansas in 1894 and later moved to Wilson County, Texas, where he married Hilda Boehme around 1920 and raised three children: Gertrude (1920), Jennive (1923), and Fred Jr. (1925).

The Lepick surname is **not** of German or Polish origin. It is **Czech/Bohemian** — consistent with the large Bohemian-Czech immigrant wave that settled in Kansas and later in Texas (the Texas Czech belt runs through Wilson, Guadalupe, and surrounding counties).

Hilda Boehme was German-Texan; her parents remain UNVERIFIED due to no surviving freely-accessible records establishing her parents with certainty.

## Answers to Specific Research Questions

### 1. Frederick Lepick Sr. — birth, parents, immigration

Frederick ("Fred") Charles Lepick Sr. was born 8 March 1894 in Kansas (most likely Brown County). He was NOT born in Texas and was NOT an immigrant himself. His father Frank Lepik was the immigrant, arriving from Bohemia in 1881. Fred Sr.'s parents were Frank Lepik (Bohemian immigrant, b. 1862) and Mary Mikeska (Bohemian immigrant, b. 1863). Fred Sr. moved to Wilson County, Texas at some point between 1910 and 1920.

**Confidence: CONFIRMED** (1895 KS census; 1910 AR census; 1930 TX census all documented in WikiTree Lepick-2)

### 2. Hilda Boehme — birth, parents, birthplace

Hilda Boehme was born approximately 1900. FamilySearch has a tree entry for her (PID LKM6-W7J) as "Hilda Boehme 1900–1945." Her parents are listed in FamilySearch private tree data but were not accessible from public sources during this research session. Her death around 1945 is consistent with her children being young adults and no further records of her appearing.

**Confidence: POSSIBLE** (FamilySearch PID exists; dates consistent; parents UNVERIFIED)

### 3. Boehme surname — German Texas family branch

CONFIRMED. The Boehme surname is German, meaning "Bohemian" (from Middle High German "Böhme"). The surname is distinct from the Czech/Bohemian Lepík surname. Hilda Boehme was almost certainly from the German-Lutheran settler community in Wilson County, centered at the Waldheim community (4 miles south of Floresville). The Waldheim German Lutheran Church was founded January 15, 1899, at a congregation organized in Carl Voight Jr.'s home, serving Floresville, Poth, Camp Ranch, and surroundings. The 1947 Floresville Chronicle-Journal records Lutheran services in German at Floresville Lutheran Church, confirming German-speaking community into the mid-20th century.

**Confidence: PROBABLE** (community context confirmed; Hilda's specific family origins within this community UNVERIFIED)

### 4. Czech-Bohemian vs. Polish-German immigration — which route?

The Lepick line came via the **Czech/Bohemian** route, not the Polish-German route. Frank Lepik emigrated from Bohemia (Austria-Hungary) in 1881 and settled in Brown County, Kansas — part of the large Czech-Bohemian immigrant wave that settled in northeastern Kansas in the 1870s-1880s. This is a well-documented migration path: Bohemian Czechs → Hamburg or Bremen → New York or Baltimore → Kansas by rail. The Mikeska family (Czech) came via the same corridor, arriving in 1871.

The Lepick family came to Texas indirectly: Kansas (1880s-1901) → Arkansas (1901-~1920) → Wilson County, Texas (~1920s) → San Antonio (~1930s). They did NOT come to Texas in the 1880s wave of Czech-Bohemian immigrants that settled directly in the Texas Czech belt (Fayette, Austin, Lavaca counties). They arrived later.

The Boehme family (Hilda's line) was part of the **German Lutheran** immigrant stream to South Texas, which was a separate migration from the Czech stream.

**Confidence: CONFIRMED** for Lepick Czech origin; **PROBABLE** for Boehme German Texas origin

### 5. Other Lepick siblings, cousins, and Wilson County records

Frank Lepik Sr. had 9 documented children (generation 3 section above). Of these, Fred Sr. (generation 2) is the one who moved to Texas. The other siblings remained primarily in Kansas and Arkansas. Their documented descendants in the wider Lepick family include the Blacketer family line (Mary A. Lepick married James W. Blacketer in Brown County, Kansas 1908) and the Ohio/New Jersey Lepick clusters visible in the SSA death records.

**Wilson County TX records** (from the 1930 census citation): The Lepick household in Wilson County in 1930 is confirmed by FamilySearch Image Group 004953946, showing the family in Wilson County. No other Lepick household appears to have been present in Wilson County — Fred Sr. was the only Lepick sibling to migrate to Texas.

## Surname Etymology Note

The surname **Lepick** (and its variants Lepik, Lepický, Lípek) is of Czech/Bohemian origin. The most likely etymological root is from a craftsman word, possibly related to a trade name. The 1884 Atchison newspaper described Frank Lepik as "an enterprising young mechanic," consistent with a craftsman family tradition. Frank later ran a boot & shoe shop in Horton, Kansas — a skilled trade typical of Czech immigrant tradesmen.

The surname spelling standardized from "Lepik" to "Lepick" over generations in Kansas and Texas.
