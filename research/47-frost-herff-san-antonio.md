# Frost / Herff / Chabot / San Antonio Notable Maternal-Side Research

**Compiled:** 2026-04-26  
**Status:** Focused collateral research. This memo documents the Frost/Herff/Kampmann San Antonio network and separates it from Shari's confirmed maternal line.

## Bottom Line

Shari Mattingly's documented mother was **Jennive Imogene Lepick**, not a Frost or Herff. Jennive's obituary names her as the mother of **Sharyn Mattingly** and gives her maiden name and parents. That means no source-backed Frost/Herff descent is currently established for Shari's maternal line. [1]

What *is* source-backed is a real San Antonio notable-family cluster:

- **Thomas Claiborne Frost (1833-1903)** founded what became Frost National Bank. [2]
- **Ferdinand Ludwig Herff (1820-1912)** was an early German-Texan physician who reached Texas in **1847** and moved to San Antonio in **1850**. [3]
- **John Herman Kampmann (1819-1885)** reached San Antonio in **1848** and became one of its major 19th-century builders and businessmen. [4]
- **Florence Ilse Herff Frost (1906-1984)** belonged to the Herff/Kampmann network and was the mother of **Tom Frost Jr.**, the fourth-generation Frost Bank leader. [5][6][7][8]

So the Frost/Herff story is historically real, but it is **not the confirmed Lepick maternal line**.

## Confirmed: Shari's Maternal Line Is Lepick, Not Frost

- Jennive Imogene Lepick Mattingly's published obituary states that she was born on **February 2, 1923**, in **Floresville, Texas**, to **Frederick and Hilda (Boehme) Lepick**.
- The same obituary names her surviving children, including **Sharyn Mattingly**. [1]

This is the cleanest source-backed reason the older Frost-mother hypothesis should stay rejected.

## Confirmed: The Frost / Herff / Kampmann San Antonio Network

### 1. Frost bank founder

**Thomas Claiborne Frost** was born in Alabama on **December 31, 1833**, arrived in Texas in **1854**, later settled in San Antonio, and founded the banking enterprise that became **Frost National Bank**. TSHA states that after his death in **1903**, the presidency passed first to his son **Thomas C. Frost** and then to another son, **Joseph H. Frost**. [2]

### 2. Early Herff arrival in Texas

**Ferdinand Ludwig von Herff** was born in Darmstadt, Germany, on **November 29, 1820**. TSHA states that he helped organize the **Bettina** colony in Texas in **1847**, returned to Germany, then came back to Texas permanently, settling first in New Braunfels and then moving to **San Antonio in 1850**. TSHA also credits him as a pioneer physician and says he was instrumental in establishing San Antonio's first hospital. [3]

### 3. Early Kampmann arrival in San Antonio

**John Herman Kampmann** was born in Prussia on **December 25, 1819**, and TSHA states that he **left Europe in 1848 and settled in San Antonio**. He became a major builder, contractor, hotel owner, and businessman; TSHA specifically notes his work on the **Menger Hotel** and his role in San Antonio civic development. [4]

### 4. Kampmann daughter Eda and the Herff branch

TSHA identifies **Eda** as one of John Herman Kampmann's children. [4]  
The descendant-published family-history book *Eda and Ilse* identifies **Eda Kampmann Herff** as Kampmann's daughter and states that her granddaughter was **Ilse Herff Frost**. It further states that Ilse was the daughter of Eda's son "Johnny" / Dr. John Herff. [6]

Judith Carrington's San Antonio Report essay, written from the same family archive behind *Eda and Ilse*, likewise identifies **Eda Kampmann Herff** as **John Kampmann's daughter** and describes the Ilse memoir section as written by **Kampmann's granddaughter**. [7]

### 5. John Bennett Herff and Florence Ilse Herff

John Benedict's local-history article on Caleb Thomas's log cabin states that **Dr. John Bennett Herff (1878-1959)** was the **son of Dr. John Adolph Herff** and the **grandson of Dr. Ferdinand Ludwig Herff**. It also states that John B. Herff married **Florence Harris** in **1902** and that their children were:

- **Carol** (1904-1922)
- **Florence Ilse** (1906-1984)
- **Jean** (1910-1996) [5]

That gives a source-backed placement for **Florence Ilse Herff** inside the Herff line.

### 6. Florence Ilse Herff Frost and Tom Frost Jr.

In *Rivers, Ranches, Railroads, & Recreation*, **Ilse Herff Frost** wrote that:

- her father was **Dr. John Herff**
- the ranch came down from **Dr. Ferdinand Ludwig von Herff**
- her husband's grandfather, **Col. T. C. Frost**, founded Frost National Bank
- her son **Tom** was chief executive of the Frost banking family in San Antonio. [8]

Tom Frost Jr.'s obituary independently states that he was born on **October 29, 1927**, in San Antonio to **Thomas Clayborne Frost** and **Ilsé Herff Frost**, and that he was the **fourth generation to lead Frost Bank**. [9]

Taken together, the accessible sources support this San Antonio notable-family chain:

1. **John Herman Kampmann** (arrived San Antonio 1848) [4]  
2. his daughter **Eda Kampmann Herff** [4][6][7]  
3. her son **Dr. John Herff / John Bennett Herff** as preserved in family-history and local-history sources [5][6]  
4. **Florence Ilse Herff** (1906-1984) [5]  
5. **Tom Frost Jr.** (1927-2018), Frost Bank leader [8][9]

## Chabot: Useful Period Source, Not a Proven Family Link

**Frederick Charles Chabot (1891-1943)** was a San Antonio historian. TSHA identifies his **most notable work** as **_With the Makers of San Antonio_ (1937)**. [10]

Google Books confirms that *With the Makers of San Antonio* is a **412-page genealogy and biography compilation** of early San Antonio families, published as **Yanaguana Society Publication 4**. [11]

That makes Chabot a legitimate period source for this cluster. But in this pass I did **not** use Chabot's book to prove any exact Frost/Herff kinship, because the openly accessible scan did not give me page-level Herff/Frost passages to quote or verify directly.

## Probable, But Not Yet Source-Backed Enough To Assert As Fact

- That Shari's oral memory about a Frost Bank relative came from a real **Frost/Herff/Kampmann social-family circle** around old San Antonio, rather than from her actual maternal ancestry.
- The exact documentary step from **Eda Kampmann Herff** to **John Bennett Herff** is well supported by descendant/family-history material in this pass, but I did not independently verify it with a civil record, probate file, or cemetery record here.
- Any blood or marriage link from **Jennive Lepick** into the Frost/Herff family remains **unproven**.

## Practical Takeaway For The Project

For this genealogy site, the safest treatment is:

- keep **Jennive Lepick** as Shari's confirmed mother
- treat the **Frost/Herff/Kampmann** material as an investigated **San Antonio notable-family cluster**
- only connect Shari to that cluster if a new source appears that explicitly bridges **Lepick/Mattingly** to **Frost/Herff**
