## Q2: Boehme Family Settlement Region

**Lavaca County / Wilson County corridor — NOT the Hill Country Adelsverein zone.**

The Boehme family anchored in Shiner (Lavaca County) and later spread to Floresville (Wilson County), placing them squarely in the South Texas German Belt — the Lavaca/DeWitt/Wilson/Gonzales County cluster, distinct from the Fredericksburg/New Braunfels Hill Country settlements.

- Shiner, Lavaca County: Founded largely by Czech and German immigrants in the 1880s–1900s. German fraternal organizations including Sons of Hermann were active. TSHA: `https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/shiner`
- Floresville, Wilson County: County seat of Wilson County; Wilson County had significant German immigration from Yorktown/DeWitt County westward. TSHA: `https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/wilson-county`
- Yorktown (DeWitt County): Key staging point for German settlers moving into Wilson and Lavaca counties. TSHA: `https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/yorktown`

Source: TSHA Online Handbook of Texas — Wilson County, Shiner, Yorktown entries (accessed 2026-04-26). **CONFIRMED** for geographic settlement pattern.

## Q3: Adelsverein vs. Post-1848 Immigration Wave

**Assessment: Post-1848 wave, approximately 1855–1862 — PROBABLE**

Herman F. Boehme was born June 9, 1863 in Texas. If his parents arrived as a young couple or just before starting a family:
- Adelsverein wave (1845–1847): Would put parents arriving ~18 years before Herman's birth — possible but would imply a very late child.
- Post-1848 (Forty-Eighters + 1850s-1860s wave): Galveston and Indianola were the primary entry ports. The 1850s saw large German immigration to Lavaca/DeWitt/Victoria County areas via Indianola (Port Lavaca area). This timeline fits Herman's 1863 birth naturally.

**Lavaca County geography confirms post-Adelsverein:** Adelsverein settlements concentrated in Gillespie, Kendall, and Comal counties (the Hill Country). Lavaca/Wilson County German settlers arrived predominantly via the 1850s–1860s Galveston/Indianola wave, not the Adelsverein colonization scheme.

The Boehme name itself is geographically distributed across Saxony, Bohemia, and the North German Plain — the name does not narrow the origin province definitively. However, the Lavaca County settlement context is most consistent with Westphalian or Lower Saxony origins (common for that migration corridor) rather than Bohemian Sudeten German.

Sources:
- TSHA Handbook: Adelsverein entry `https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/adelsverein`
- TSHA Handbook: Shiner, Wilson County, Yorktown entries
- PeopleLegacy data: Herman born 1863 TX (anchor point for immigration timing estimate)

## Q5: Church Affiliation

**Lutheran — PROBABLE**

Evidence:
1. Herman F. Boehme buried at **Shiner Sons of Herman Cemetery** — Sons of Hermann (Söhne Hermanns) was founded in San Antonio 1861, modeled on German Protestant fraternal traditions. Membership strongly correlated with Lutheran affiliation among South Texas Germans. TSHA: `https://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/entries/sons-of-hermann`
2. Sibling **Frieda Boehme** buried at **Saint Johns Lutheran Cemetery, Poth, Wilson County TX** — direct Lutheran burial record for Herman's child. CONFIRMED (PeopleLegacy).
3. Hilda, Dora, and Annie buried at **Floresville City Cemetery** (non-denominational municipal cemetery) — consistent with either Lutheran or no formal church at time of burial. Neither confirms nor contradicts.
4. Anomaly: Edgar Charles Boehme buried at **Shiner Catholic Cemetery** — possibly converted via marriage; does not negate Lutheran pattern for rest of family.

**Verdict:** The Boehme family was Lutheran by tradition, consistent with German Protestant immigrants in this region. Edgar's Catholic burial is an individual divergence, not a family pattern.

## Settlement Context Summary

The Boehme family fits the **South Texas German Belt** pattern:

- Entry: Galveston or Indianola port, ~1855–1862
- Initial settlement: Lavaca County (Shiner area), farming/labor community
- Fraternal affiliation: Sons of Hermann (German Protestant mutual aid)
- Church: Lutheran (Saint Johns Lutheran, Poth; consistent with Wilson/Lavaca County German churches)
- Second-generation drift: Lavaca County → Wilson County (Floresville) via marriage/economic migration, 1900–1945
- Geographic contrast: NOT the Adelsverein Hill Country (Fredericksburg/New Braunfels zone)
