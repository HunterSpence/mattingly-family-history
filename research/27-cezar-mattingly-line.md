## Summary

Cezar (also spelled Caesar or Cesar) Mattingly was the younger brother of Hunter Spence's direct ancestor Thomas Mattingly II. He co-received the 300-acre "Mattingly's Hope" land patent in Charles County, Maryland on 4 September 1666. His verified line runs six generations to the Garrett County, Maryland branch — centered on the village of Hoyes (now a hamlet in southern Garrett County). The Hoye appendix in the 1969 book covers this branch in detail. The paternal Y-DNA haplogroup for Cezar's male-line descendants is confirmed as R-Y14083 (a sub-branch of R1b-DF27), verified by FamilyTreeDNA testing of known Mattingly paternal descendants.

## Cezar Mattingly — Generation 1

**Cezar (Caesar/Cesar) Mattingly**
- Born: after 1654, Berkshire, England (WikiTree Mattingly-223)
- Died: after 1719, St. Mary's County, Maryland (last record 1719; colonial-settlers-md-va.us gives "between 1719 and 1720")
- Spouse: Jane Suttle (dates unknown)
- Co-patentee with Thomas II of "Mattingly's Hope," 300 acres, Charles County, Maryland, 4 September 1666
- The 1969 book's Hoye Appendix (published 1936 in the Mountain Democrat as "The Mattingly Family") confirms Cezar as one of the children who emigrated: "Thomas Mattingly, his wife, Elizabeth, and children, Thomas, Cezar and Elizabeth, came to Maryland prior to 1665"
- Cezar retained the Mattingly's Hope Charles County tract; Thomas II later acquired land in St. Mary's County ("Mount Misery")
- His given name is unusual in an English Catholic family; it may reflect Continental Catholic influence in Lord Baltimore's colonial circle
- WikiTree biography source: colonial-settlers-md-va.us person ID I007617

**Cezar's two confirmed children (WikiTree Mattingly-223):**
1. Thomas Mattingly (b. abt.1688) — Mattingly-928; no confirmed spouse or children in accessible records
2. John Baptist Mattingly (b. 1690, Maryland) — Mattingly-225; the line that reaches Garrett County

## Generation 6: Dominick Mattingly (1808-1854) — Founder of the Hoyes Settlement

**Dominick Mattingly** — the Garrett County anchor
- WikiTree: Mattingly-648
- Born: 6 March 1808, Somerset County, Pennsylvania
- Died: 27 May 1854, Hoyes (then "Johnstown"), Garrett County, Maryland
- Spouse: Ann Browning (b. January 1812, d. after 1899), daughter of Meshack Browning, the "famous hunter"
- Married 1 March 1832; license issued 22 February 1832 at Cumberland, MD

**Arrival narrative (1969 book, Hoye Appendix, p.232):**
In January 1831, Dominick and his brother John Mattingly (son of Henry II) bought 157 acres on the Youghiogheny River above the mouth of Sang Run, MD, from John Hoye for $196.56. They cleared the land; John built a house on the hillside and Dominick built by a spring. On 17 December 1853, Dominick bought Catherine Townshend's 386-acre farm at Johnstown (now Hoyes) for $2,500. He died the following May 1854, leaving his widow and children on this property.

**Dominick's children (1969 book, confirmed):**
1. Mary F. Mattingly (b. 11 February 1833) — married Frank Sebold, 29 August 1854
2. Anne Celestine Mattingly (b. 29 September 1834, d. 20 February 1899) — married William DeWitt
3. Meshack Mattingly (b. 23 June 1837, d. 1 May 1912) — Garrett County Commissioner
4. William H. Mattingly (b. 11 February 1840, d. 28 January 1863) — died young, buried Hoyes
5. Elizabeth E. Mattingly (b. 3 April 1843, d. 1 April 1871) — buried Hoyes
6. Dominick Mattingly (b. 22 September 1845) — confirmed at Cross Roads 1855; no further record

## The Hoyes Community and St. Dominick Church

The village of Johnstown, Garrett County, was renamed "Hoyes" in 1880 in honor of Edward Hoye (Dominick's neighbor and the author Charles E. Hoye's ancestor). The Mattingly homestead stood opposite the Catholic church. Dominick Mattingly, Meshack Browning, and William Browning collected donations and built St. James Church (dedicated 1853), later renamed St. Dominick in 1890 by Father Romanus Mattingly in honor of Dominick Mattingly, "who was a zealous Catholic." The church and old St. Dominic's Cemetery at Hoyes contain the graves of Dominick, his children, grandchildren, and several of his sons-in-law.

- Son of Dominick; born at Sang Run, Garrett County

- Son of Meshack and Mary Griffith; early life at Hoyes
- Baptized St. Peter's, Oakland, MD

### Rev. Romanus Mattingly (1850-1922) — the Priest Who Built the Church
**Important: This Romanus is from the Henry I / John Mattingly (of HENRY I) line, not from Dominick's line.**
- Son of Sylvester Mattingly (1817-1891), who was the youngest son of John Mattingly (son of Henry I)
- Born 29 August 1850; ordained June 1878 by Cardinal Gibbons
- Assigned to Oakland parish 10 September 1882; served 16 years
- Built current Catholic churches at Oakland and Hoyes
- Changed the church name from St. James to St. Dominick in honor of Dominick Mattingly
- Father Stanton: "Father Mattingly is blest with a good mind and a great deal of natural ability"
- Died 19 April 1922, St. Agnes Hospital, Baltimore; buried Bonnie Brae Cemetery, Baltimore
- His brother: Alexander Mattingly of Mt. Savage (survived him)

## The Hoye Appendix — Source Assessment

The Hoye appendix in the 1969 book is Chapter LXXX of Charles E. Hoye's "Garrett County History of Pioneer Families," originally published 7 May 1936 in the "Mountain Democrat" newspaper. Charles E. Hoye was a Garrett County local historian whose family owned the farm adjoining the Mattingly homestead at Hoyes. The village was renamed after his ancestor Edward Hoye. Hoye had direct access to the Dominick Mattingly family Bible records, local church registers at St. Peter's Oakland and St. Dominic's Hoyes, and the Cumberland Court House records. His data on births and marriages matches the church records cited in the 1969 book — the marriage license dates, baptism dates, and probate records all corroborate each other.

**Confidence level:** HIGH for Dominick Mattingly's family and descendants (three independent sources: family Bible, church records, courthouse records). MODERATE for the Cezar → John Baptist → John (1715) path (WikiTree only; not independently confirmed in the 1969 book text, which does not trace Henry I back to Cezar explicitly). The path is internally consistent across WikiTree sources with no contradictions found.

## Open Questions

1. **Cezar's will or probate record**: A will for Cezar (d.1719-1720) should exist in St. Mary's County or Charles County probate records. The colonial-settlers-md-va.us entry lists his death "between 1719 and 1720, St. Mary's County, Maryland." The Maryland State Archives (mdsa.net) Hall of Records in Annapolis holds Charles County Wills from this period. This would confirm his children definitively.

2. **Thomas Mattingly (Mattingly-928, b.1688, Cezar's other son)**: No spouse or children are recorded on WikiTree. If he had issue, that would add a second branch from Cezar.

3. **Confirmation of John (b.1715) as Henry I's father**: The 1969 book cites "Henry Wright Newman" as the source for Henry I being "son of John Mattingly, St. Mary's County." WikiTree confirms John (Mattingly-258, b.1715) as Henry I's father. Newman's work is unpublished family research; a primary source (Henry I's baptism record or a will naming him as John's son) would elevate this from PROBABLE to CONFIRMED.

4. **Don Mattingly genealogy**: If Hunter wants to establish a cousin relationship with Don Mattingly (baseball), a dedicated search of his Evansville/Indiana Mattingly ancestry is needed. No connection found in this research pass.

## Key Locations

- **Mattingly's Hope, Charles County, MD**: The original 300-acre 1666 land patent. No modern village persists under that name.
- **Hoyes, Garrett County, MD**: A hamlet in southern Garrett County near the Youghiogheny River. St. Dominic's Cemetery contains the graves of Dominick Mattingly, Meshack Mattingly, William Mattingly Sr., and their families.
- **Mount Savage, Allegany County, MD**: Henry Mattingly I's home; still a community west of Cumberland.
- **Sang Run, Garrett County, MD**: Dominick's first Maryland farm, 1831-1853.
- **Oakland, Garrett County, MD**: Parish seat; St. Peter's Church holds baptism and confirmation records.
