## 2. The Critical Surname Question: How Hunter Got "Spence"

### Finding (UNVERIFIED — awaits family confirmation)

The task brief notes that Hunter's mother is **Rachel** — daughter of Sharyn "Shari" Mattingly and **David Spence**. Since Rachel is a Mattingly by birth, she would not carry the Spence surname automatically. Two scenarios explain how Hunter bears the Spence name:

**Scenario A (PROBABLE):** Rachel kept or legally adopted her maiden name "Spence" (e.g., after divorce from her father, after Shari's separation from David, or as an adult choice), and Hunter inherited that name from Rachel. This is the most common mechanism when grandchildren carry a maternal grandfather's surname — the mother reclaimed her maiden name as her legal name, and her child took it.

**Scenario B (POSSIBLE):** Hunter's biological father (Rachel's husband or partner) independently carries the surname Spence. This would be a coincidence, but it is not impossible — Spence is the 894th most common US surname with approximately 48,000 bearers.

**Assessment:** The task explicitly asks which scenario applies. No public vital records confirm either. From family context, Shari's obituary trail shows "Sharyn Mattingly of Santa Monica, California" — no reference to a married daughter named Spence. The most parsimonious explanation is Scenario A: Rachel uses Spence as her legal surname (either her birth surname never changed, or she reclaimed it), and Hunter's surname flows through her. This requires family confirmation to resolve.

**Significance:** Under Scenario A, Hunter's Spence surname traces patrilineally through David Spence but reaches him via maternal transmission — making it a matrilineal descent of a paternal surname, which is genealogically significant but not unusual in divorced or single-parent family structures.

**Important:** None of David W. Spence's children produced a male Spence line that would reach to Hunter's grandfather. The direct male line died with Alvin "Bud" Spence in 1944. This family is presented as documented context for Texas Spence settlement, not as Hunter's direct ancestors.

**David W. Spence's Illinois origin:** The 1840 US Census for Cass County, Illinois contains an entry for "David Spence" as head of household. Since David W. Spence (born 19 November 1840) was born in that exact county that year, the census head of household is David W.'s father — the elder David Spence. This places the Spence family in Cass County, Illinois by 1840, consistent with a migration from the Virginia → Kentucky → Illinois corridor.

## 5. The Spence Surname — Clan Origin and Historical Documentation

### 5A. Clan Spens (CONFIRMED — Wikipedia, documented Scottish clan records)

The Spence / Spens name in Scotland traces to a documented Lowland clan. Key findings:

- **Clan Spens** is a recognized Lowland Scottish clan, classified as a sept of **Clan MacDuff**. The current chief is Patrick Nathaniel George Spens, 4th Baron Spens. Region: Fife.
- **Motto:** *Si Deus Quis Contra* ("If God is for us, who is against us")
- **Crest:** A hart's head erased proper

**Medieval origin (CONFIRMED — charter records):**
- John 'Dispensator' or 'Le Dispenser' appeared as a tenant/vassal of Walter fitz Alan, Steward of Scotland, 1161-1171
- Roger 'Dispensator' witnessed a charter by the Bishop of Moray between 1202-1222
- Thomas 'Dispensator' (son of Roger) witnessed a charter in 1232
- John Spens listed as baillie of Irvine, 1260
- **Henry de Spens**, feudal baron, swore fealty to Edward I of England and appears on the **Ragman Rolls of 1296** — the first Spens individual in the historical record by surname
- Thomas de Spens (Henry's son) witnessed Edinburgh charters 1296-1324
- **Bishop Thomas Spens** (15th century): appointed Bishop of Galloway, later Bishop of Aberdeen; Lord Privy Seal until 1470; royal ambassador; buried Edinburgh 1480

**The French branch (CONFIRMED — Wikipedia):**
Patrick Spens (youngest son of John Spens of Lathallan) was sent by King James II of Scotland to King Charles VII of France in 1450. He settled in France and founded the Barony de Spens d'Estignols. The Spens men served in the *Garde Écossaise* — the personal bodyguard of the French kings from the mid-15th century until the French Revolution (1789).

### 5B. The Orkney Spence Family — Documented since 1600s (CONFIRMED — Kirkwall OPR records)

The Orkney Family History Society (orkneyfhs.co.uk) has published a detailed documented genealogy of the Spence of Scapa family in Kirkwall, Orkney, derived from Kirkwall Old Parish Registers (OPR film 0990503, 1652-1727).

Key documented individuals include:
- Harie Spence, 1st of Scapa (pre-1657)
- George Spence, 2nd of Scapa (baillie of Kirkwall) — multiple baptism records 1657-1663
- Patrick Spence, merchant and town treasurer of Kirkwall
- **David Spence, 5th of Scapa** — documented in the Scapa pedigree
- William Spence, notary and town clerk of Kirkwall

**Significance for Basil Spence:** Sir Basil Urwin Spence (1907-1976), the architect of Coventry Cathedral, had "Orcadian parents" per the Edinburgh University Our History database. His father Urwin Archibald Spence worked for the Royal Mint. The family connection to Kirkwall Orcadian Spences is documented in principle but not traced in a direct pedigree in accessible free sources.

## 6. Military Service — Spence Ancestors

### 6A. Civil War (POSSIBLE for Navarro County line; UNVERIFIED for Hunter's line)

David W. Spence (1840-1907) lived in Navarro County, Texas during the Civil War (1861-1865) and was aged 20-24 during the conflict. He would have been eligible for Confederate service. **No specific Civil War record was found for him** in the accessible Navarro County rosters. His son Alvin "Bud" Spence (1880-1944) would have been eligible for WWI but died with no known military record.

### 6B. WWI (NOT FOUND)

A search of Navarro County WWII veterans lists (navarrocountyhistory.com) found no Spence entries. WWI records were not searched in this session.

### 6C. WWII (CONFIRMED — Benjamin "Ben" Spence)

Benjamin "Ben" Spence (born Wilson County, Texas, 10 July 1905) is **confirmed to have served in the US military** by virtue of his burial at **Fort Sam Houston National Cemetery** (Plot 3051286, confirmed on Find a Grave memorial #3051286 per Smokykin source S147). Fort Sam Houston National Cemetery requires military service for burial eligibility.

Service branch and unit unknown from accessible free records. His death (6 July 1951) was ruled a self-inflicted gunshot wound.

### 6D. Navarro County Spence in Civil War — Research Gap

## 7. Surname Origin: Scotland vs. England vs. Ireland

Based on this research, the Spence surname in America arrived via multiple independent immigrant streams:

| Stream | Origin | Route to America | Arrival | Confidence |
|--------|--------|-----------------|---------|------------|
| Scottish-Fife colonial | Glasgow/Dysart, Fife, Scotland | Scotland → Virginia colonial ~1660s | 1639/1679 (David Spence of Dysart) | PROBABLE |
| Clan Spens nobility | Fife (Lathallan) | Via documented Scottish history | Medieval (no American branch confirmed) | CONFIRMED (historical) |
| Irish-South Carolina | Ireland (county unknown) | Ireland → South Carolina ~1780 | ~1780-1785 (Charles Spence of Ireland) | PROBABLE |
| Orkney | Kirkwall, Orkney | Orkney → Bombay → Edinburgh | 1907 (Basil Spence's father) | CONFIRMED for Basil's family |
| Virginia colonial | Virginia/Maryland | Listed in 1623 Virginia census | 1623 (William Spence) | CONFIRMED |

**Assessment:** The Spence/Spens name is overwhelmingly Scottish in its most prominent documented line (Clan Spens of Fife, Ragman Rolls 1296), but arrived in America from multiple directions including Ireland and England. For Hunter's specific line, without confirmed documentation of David Spence's parents and birthplace, the specific national origin cannot be determined. The most common US Spence pattern (Virginia → Kentucky → Illinois → Texas) points toward the Scottish colonial Virginia stream (David Spence of Dysart, Fife, 1639 → Maryland/NC), but this is a general correlation, not a confirmed direct connection.

### Clan Spens — Baron Spens (CONFIRMED — Wikipedia)
The current chief of Clan Spens is **Patrick Nathaniel George Spens, 4th Baron Spens**. The Baron Spens title has been hereditary since the UK peerage creation in 1959.

### Sir Basil Spence — Orcadian Heritage (CONFIRMED — Edinburgh University records)

Whether Hunter's grandfather David descends from this Scottish stock (via the Virginia colonial stream, the Irish-South Carolina stream, or independently) remains unconfirmed. The surname itself is Scottish in its primary identity.

### Bishop Thomas Spens (1400s) — Scottish Church History (CONFIRMED — Wikipedia)
Bishop of Aberdeen; Lord Privy Seal of Scotland; royal ambassador for James II; buried Edinburgh 1480. There is an effigy of Bishop Spens in **Rosslyn Chapel** (the chapel made famous by *The Da Vinci Code*), suggesting a historical connection between Clan Spens and this location.
