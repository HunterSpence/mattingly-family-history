# Teichmueller / Kellner / Mollenhauer / Girsewald Expanded Research

**Research date:** 2026-04-26  
**Scope:** Verify deceased ancestors, notable collateral branches, and source-backed German/Texas records for the Teichmueller / Teichmüller, Kellner, Mollenhauer, and Girsewald lines.  
**Method:** This pass keeps only what I could tie to accessible institutional or published sources. I separate confirmed facts from narrower inferences.

---

## Executive Summary

1. **Hans Teichmueller (1837-1901) and Gustav Teichmüller (1832-1888) are confirmed brothers.** The strongest source is the 2016 *Neue Deutsche Biographie* article for Gustav, which explicitly names Hans, Gustav, Wilhelm, and Wilhelmina (Minette) as siblings and identifies Hans's wife as **Auguste Kellner**.[1]
2. **The Teichmüller patriline can be pushed back, with source support, to the late 1500s.** The same NDB article states that the family is traceable to **Hans / Johann Teichmüller (ca. 1580-1638)**, a master miller in the southern Harz, and then names later ancestors **Joachim Andreas Teichmüller (1705-1778)**, **Wilhelm Ernst Conrad Teichmüller (1758-1835)**, and **August Wilhelm Teichmüller (1795-1855)**.[1][2][3][4]
3. **The Girsewald connection is also confirmed one generation further back than earlier repo notes suggested.** Charlotte Georgine Elisabeth, mother of Hans and Gustav, is identified by NDB as the daughter of **Georg von Girsewald (1735-1816)** and **Christine Elisabeth Boyer**.[1][5]
4. **The Texas branch is well anchored.** TSHA confirms Hans's birth in Braunschweig on **March 7, 1837**, his migration to Texas after his father's death, his marriage to **Augusta Kellner** in Fayette County, his five children, his judicial career, and his death at La Grange on **February 17, 1901**.[6]
5. **The Kellner line is only partly expanded in this pass.** Augusta/Auguste Kellner is securely documented as Hans's wife, and her father is described by TSHA as a **modern-language teacher** who taught Hans English. I did **not** find an accessible institutional source in this pass naming that father or extending the Kellner line further.[6][7]

## Confirmed Siblings and Notable Collateral Branches

### 1. Gustav Teichmüller branch

**Gustav Teichmüller (1832-1888)** is not a speculative match anymore. NDB explicitly places him as Hans's brother and gives the same parents. He is the strongest "notable branch" in the German line: philosopher, historian of philosophy, and professor at Dorpat/Tartu.[1]

NDB also identifies Gustav's daughter **Anna Teichmüller (1861-1940)** as a **composer, pianist, and music teacher**, which creates a documented artistic branch off the same sibling set.[1]

### 2. Wilhelmina (Minette) Teichmüller / Mollenhauer branch

NDB explicitly identifies **Wilhelmina (Minette) Teichmüller (1829-1886)** as Hans's sister and states that she married **Karl Mollenhauer (1818-1864)**, described there as **Pastor** and **Superintendent in Bockenem, Ambergau**.[1]

This is enough to confirm a real **Mollenhauer ecclesiastical branch** attached to the family, even though I did not extend that branch to later descendants in this pass.

### 3. Wilhelm Teichmüller branch

NDB identifies another brother, **Wilhelm Teichmüller (1834-1869)**, as **Premier-Lieutenant** and **writer**. That gives the family a second documented German collateral line outside Texas.[1]

### 4. Texas art branch: Minette Teichmueller / Pohl

The **Smithville Post Office** mural record is straightforward: the New Deal Art Registry identifies **"The Law--Texas Rangers"** as a **1940 oil-on-canvas mural** by **Minette Teichmueller**, created under the **Treasury Section of Fine Arts**.[8][9]

What I can say with high confidence:

- A documented Texas artist named **Minette Teichmueller** painted the Smithville federal mural.[8][9]
- Hans and Augusta had **five children** according to TSHA.[6]

What remains slightly weaker in this pass:

- Secondary art-market biographies identify the muralist as **the daughter of Judge Hans Teichmueller and Augusta Kellner**.[10]
- I did not, in this pass, pull a page-image or institutional vital record that independently proves that parent-child link.

So the right wording here is: **the muralist Minette Teichmueller is very likely the same family member long associated with the Hans Teichmueller branch, but the parent-child link should still be tightened with a direct census, cemetery, or vital record before being treated as closed proof.**[6][8][10]

## Best Source-Backed Texas Record Set Located

### A. TSHA Handbook entry for Hans Teichmueller

This is the cleanest Texas summary source currently accessible online. It gives:

- Hans's exact birth date: **March 7, 1837**
- birthplace: **Braunschweig, Germany**
- migration context after his father's death
- marriage to **Augusta Kellner** in **1858**
- note that her father taught Hans English
- statement that the couple had **five children**
- legal and judicial career in Fayette County
- death in La Grange on **February 17, 1901**[6]

### B. 1908 memorial volume prepared by Auguste Kellner Teichmueller

UNT's Portal to Texas History has a digitized memorial volume titled **_Hans Teichmueller; biographical sketch, addresses and letters to his family_**. The metadata credits **Teichmueller, Auguste Kellner** as creator and says it was prepared by his "loving wife and daughters." This is a strong Texas family record and a direct link back to the Kellner spouse line.[7]

### C. 1902 Fayette County history

The 1902 county history **_Fayette County, Her History and Her People_** is online through Internet Archive / Library of Congress metadata and is also cited by TSHA in its bibliography for Hans. I am listing it as a priority primary source for further page-level extraction, but I am not over-claiming details from it here unless independently confirmed above.[6][11]

### D. New Deal / federal art record for Minette

The New Deal Art Registry entry for the Smithville Post Office is the best clean institutional confirmation for the Texas art branch: title, medium, year, program, location, and artist name are all explicit.[8][9]

## Best Source-Backed German Record Set Located

The backbone for the German side is **Heiner Schwenke's 2016 NDB article on Gustav Teichmüller** in Deutsche Biographie. It is doing a lot of the heavy lifting because it supplies, in one scholarly genealogy:

- the late-1500s Harz origin of the family
- four generations of named Teichmüller ancestors before Hans
- Hans and Gustav as brothers
- Charlotte's maiden line to **Georg von Girsewald** and **Christine Elisabeth Boyer**
- the sibling **Wilhelmina (Minette)** and her husband **Karl Mollenhauer**
- Gustav's daughter **Anna Teichmüller** as a notable musical descendant[1]

Deutsche Biographie also provides separate authority/index pages for **August Wilhelm Teichmüller**, **Wilhelm Ernst Conrad Teichmüller**, **Joachim Andreas Teichmüller**, and **Georg von Girsewald**, which helps stabilize names, dates, and occupations for downstream use.[2][3][4][5]

## What Is Still Open

1. **Kellner parentage**: I have not yet identified Augusta Kellner's father by name, only by profession via TSHA.[6]
2. **Direct proof that muralist Minette is Hans's daughter**: highly plausible and supported by secondary art biographies, but still worth locking down with a census, probate, cemetery, or death record.[10]
3. **Mollenhauer descendants**: Karl Mollenhauer's role is confirmed, but the branch is not extended here.[1]
4. **Further Girsewald expansion**: Georg von Girsewald is now confirmed. The next step would be archival or published noble genealogy work on the Girsewald/Boyer line rather than broad web searching.[1][5]
