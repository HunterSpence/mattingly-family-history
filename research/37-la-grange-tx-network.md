## PART VII — LA GRANGE COMMUNITY CONTEXT

### German-Czech Settlement Pattern

Per TSHA and Lotto 1902:
- 1832: First German community in county (Biegel Settlement)
- 1840s: Major German immigration to Fayette County
- 1843: Adelsverein (German colonization society) establishes Nassau Farm
- 1856: First Bohemian-Czech settlement in Texas, Dubina, founded in Fayette County
- 1880s: Second wave of German and Bohemian immigration; by 1890, ~25% of county was foreign-born (Germany 3,667; Austria-Hungary 3,224)
- La Grange population (~1902): 2,400; described as "American, German and Bohemian; about one-third negroes" (Lotto 1902, p.328)
- Two foreign-language newspapers in La Grange: Svoboda (Czech) and La Grange Zeitung (German)
- La Grange Froesch: German singing club (Lotto 1902, p.329)

### Churches in La Grange and Fayette County

Per TSHA Fayette County article and Lotto 1902:

| Denomination | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| Episcopal | Established 1852 in county (earliest churches: Methodist 1838, Baptist 1839, Presbyterian 1841, Episcopal 1852) |
| **St. James Episcopal Church, La Grange** | The Mattingly family church (Ed Jr. was Senior Warden) — CONFIRMED |
| Catholic | Heavy presence among Czech/German settlers; St. Joseph's and St. John's (Fayetteville area) |
| Lutheran | Dominant among German-Wendish settlers (county had 60 churches by late 1900s; "Lutheran and Catholic accounted for half") |
| Methodist, Baptist, Presbyterian | Active in county from 1830s |
| German Lutheran (Waldeck) | 14 miles north of La Grange, specifically German Lutheran community |
| Schulenburg | Predominantly Catholic; Rev. Father Madius; other denominations in shared "Union church" |

**For the Mattingly family specifically**: Ed Sr. (born Missouri, Protestant background from Christie/Missouri side) married into the Teichmueller family (German Lutheran tradition from Brunswick; Hans was raised by a family that attended gymnasium of Blankenburg and planned theology — likely Lutheran). The family church by Ed Jr.'s era was **Episcopal (St. James)**. Hans Teichmueller's own church affiliation is not directly stated in Lotto 1902 or TSHA — his intellectual profile ("classical scholar," wrote on American jurisprudence, a Democrat) suggests secular orientation in public life; he is not identified as a church officer in Lotto.

## PART VIII — QUESTIONS ANSWERED

### Q1: Edward Mattingly Sr.'s exact arrival in TX — when and from where in MO?

**PROBABLE:** Edward was born 6 Sep 1868 in Missouri to Thomas J. Mattingly and Elizabeth Christie Mattingly. His mother died 1876 in Daviess County MO when Ed was ~8. He presumably migrated to Texas sometime between 1876 and 1894 (he married May Teichmueller on 1 Sep 1894). He was working as assistant cashier of the First National Bank by at least 1894 when he took that position (Holloway bio: "Accepted the position of cashier ... on January 8, 1894" — Ed presumably joined around the same time or shortly after). His exact year of arrival in TX is UNVERIFIED from free sources; the 1880 US Census for Texas would confirm or deny his Texas presence at age 12.

**From where in MO:** Most likely Daviess County or Gentry County, northwest Missouri (where his mother died and is buried). NOT Kentucky (the Mattingly Kentucky Catholic line was eliminated as his parentage — his mother was from Shelby County KY but died in Daviess County MO, and his father Thomas is not connected to any KY Mattingly line in confirmed sources).

### Q2: Who else of the Mattingly clan migrated to Fayette TX?

**CONFIRMED:** Only Edward Mattingly Sr. and his direct descendants are confirmed in La Grange. His brother **Noble N. Mattingly** settled in **Concho County, TX** (Millersview), not Fayette County — a separate Texas migration. **Ida Davison** (Ed's sister, b. 1862) has no confirmed Texas connection. No other Mattinglys appear in the Lotto 1902 citizen or business sections for Fayette County.

### Q3: Did Edward Sr. own land in La Grange? Where? Plot size?

**UNVERIFIED.** No Mattingly land patent appears in GLO records for Fayette County (GLO search redirected with 404; patent database requires online form submission not accessible via automated query). The Fayette County deed records are held at the county courthouse in La Grange and are not online for this period. His burial plot (0014-07-0, adjacent to May's 0014-08-0) is in the La Grange Cemetery. His son Edward Jr. maintained a farm "near Round Top, Texas" — this may be inherited land, POSSIBLE but unconfirmed as an Ed Sr. property.

### Q4: Hans Teichmueller's death — La Grange paper obituary 1901?

**PROBABLE CONFIRMATION, NOT ACCESSIBLE.** The Portal to Texas History holds digitized Texas newspapers including the La Grange Journal, but bot protection blocks automated access. Lotto 1902 (published same year, 1902) provides the fullest available account. Lotto records: "In January 1901, the court adjourned out of respect on the death of H. Teichmueller who had filled the office of District Judge for three terms and other offices of public trust with great honor." TSHA confirms death on 17 February 1901 at La Grange. The Proceedings of the Texas Bar Association for 1901 (cited in TSHA bibliography) would contain an obituary-style memorial; not yet accessed. The 1908 biographical sketch (Von Boeckmann-Jones) is also not yet accessed. A La Grange Journal obituary from February 1901 almost certainly exists but requires direct newspaper microfilm access (Briscoe Center, UT Austin) or Portal to Texas History once human-accessible.

### Q5: Edward Sr.'s two wives — full marriage records?

- **May Teichmueller marriage**: 1 September 1894, La Grange TX. CONFIRMED per FAG bio cross-reference. Marriage record in Texas Select County Marriage Records 1837–1965 (FamilySearch collection) would confirm; not directly accessed.
- **Blanche Schroeder marriage**: Exact date UNVERIFIED from free sources; after 28 Aug 1900 (May's death) and before 21 Feb 1915 (Edward Jr.'s birth). The marriage would be in Fayette County TX marriage records 1900–1905.

### Q6: May Teichmueller's death 28 Aug 1900 — cause?

**UNVERIFIED.** Lotto 1902 says she "died two years ago" without specifying cause. The oral tradition of "died in childbirth" delivering Mamie persists but is not confirmed. Claude (b. 4 Jul 1895) and Leroy (b. 31 Aug 1896) are the confirmed children; a possible third child near 1900 could explain the childbirth theory. Texas death certificates begin 1903 — May's death in 1900 predates the statewide death registration system. Fayette County local records (if any) and a La Grange Journal notice from August–September 1900 are the best available sources; both require direct archive access.

### Q7: Edward Mattingly Jr. (b. 21 Feb 1915) — full life record?

**CONFIRMED.** See Part III above. Full obituary text from Koenig & Strickland Funeral Home, La Grange, available via FAG memorial #74964262.

### Q8: Who else lived with the Mattinglys per census years?

### Q9: Schulenburg connection — any Mattingly relatives there?

**PROBABLE NONE.** Lotto 1902 has no Mattingly in the Schulenburg section (pp. 341+). The Schulenburg community was predominantly Czech Catholic and German Catholic. Noble N. Mattingly settled in Concho County, not Schulenburg. No Mattingly surname appears in the Schulenburg citizen or business listings in Lotto 1902. UNVERIFIED whether any Mattingly relatives visited or operated in Schulenburg.

### Q10: La Grange's German-Czech church communities — which did the Mattinglys attend?

**CONFIRMED (partial)**: St. James Episcopal Church, La Grange — confirmed for Edward Mattingly Jr. (Senior Warden). The Episcopal congregation was established in Fayette County 1852 and would have been the appropriate choice for an American-origin family (Missouri Protestant background) marrying into the German community. Hans Teichmueller's church affiliation is not stated; the Teichmueller family was from the Brunswick German Protestant tradition (Hans's father was a Brunswick army officer; Hans planned to study theology — likely Lutheran background). Whether Ed Sr. joined the Episcopal or German church is not confirmed in primary sources.
